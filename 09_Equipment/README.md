# ECONOMY & GEAR

In *The Murk*, you cannot simply walk into a store and buy a machine gun. The factories of Carantha are stalling, and the trade routes of Kemet are choked by fog. Gold is heavy and useless; a man dying of thirst cannot drink a diamond.

* **Cash is dead.**
* **Barter is king.** A can of peaches is worth more than a wedding ring.

Equipment is not background flavor. What you carry determines whether you survive the next scene. Managing your gear — what you bring, what you trade, what you scrounge when it runs out — is as much the game as the violence that depletes it.

---

## 1. SCROUNGING (THE MARKET)

To find gear, you must hunt for it. This represents searching ruins, trading with nervous locals, or raiding forgotten supply caches. Scrounging uses the **[Scrounge Skill](/12_Skills/#8-scrounge-finding-equipment)**.

### THE ROLL
Roll `2d6 + MIND + Scrounge Bonus`.

| DR | Tier | Examples |
|:--|:--|:--|
| **DR 10 (Common)** | *The Basics.* | Food, Clean Water, Basic Ammo, Bandages, Alcohol. |
| **DR 15 (Specialist)** | *The Tools.* | Specific Guns, Med-Kits, Climbing Gear, Fuel, Batteries, Gas Mask Filters. |
| **DR 20 (Relics)** | *The Old World.* | Old Empire Tech, Magical Components, Heavy Weaponry, Masterwork Armor. |

### THE RISK
Scrounging takes time and exposes you.
* **Failure:** You find nothing useful.
* **Botch (Failure + "1" on Attribute Die):** You find trouble. You are accosted by thugs, disturb a nest of Murk-rats, or the item you acquire is cursed, broken, or watched.

### SCROUNGE MODIFIERS
* **Active settlement (markets, traders):** No modifier.
* **Small outpost or frontier town:** +2 DR.
* **Direct ruin looting:** +4 DR, but no Barter cost — what you find, you keep.
* **Contact in the area (Friends in Low Places):** -2 DR.
* **Taking a full Scene to search:** -2 DR.

## 2. BARTER LEVELS (THE CURRENCY)

We do not track coins. We track **Value Levels**. To buy an item, you must trade something of equal Level.

### LEVEL 1: SCRAP
* **Examples:** Rusted metal, canned food, water, simple ammo, bandages, rope, matches.
* **Buying Power:** Keeps you alive for another day.
* **Cash Equivalent:** Anything under 10 marks in the old currency. Not that marks mean anything.

### LEVEL 2: GOODS
* **Examples:** Working firearms, Med-Kits, fuel canisters, tools, functional armor, gas mask filters, Repair Kits.
* **Buying Power:** Keeps you working.
* **Cash Equivalent:** Pre-war trade goods. A gun that shoots straight. A coat that keeps out the cold.

### LEVEL 3: RELICS
* **Examples:** Old Empire technology, magical foci, secrets, heavy machinery, pristine luxuries (Coffee, Chocolate, Fine Spirits).
* **Buying Power:** Changes your life. Can buy entry into high society, a meeting with a Prince, or a safe house in a city that usually kills people like you.
* **Cash Equivalent:** The kind of thing people go into the Murk to find.

### BARTER IN PRACTICE

Trades do not have to be item-for-item. The Director adjudicates value based on **scarcity, condition, and who wants it**.

* A working firearm is Level 2 in Novemgrod — common enough. The same firearm in a starving frontier outpost that hasn't seen a supply convoy in six months? The Director may rule it's worth a Level 3 trade.
* Information is a valid commodity. Knowing the safe route through a district, or where a rival faction keeps their cache, can be Level 1 (common gossip) to Level 3 (something people have died to keep secret).
* Services trade at the Director's discretion. A doctor's work is Level 2. A master Esoterist's ritual work is Level 3.

## 3. INVENTORY (LOAD)

Your inventory is abstract. We do not count pounds; we count **Slots**. You carry what your frame can support.

### CAPACITY
* **Max Load:** Equal to your **BODY DIE value**.
    * *Body d6:* 6 Slots.
    * *Body d8:* 8 Slots.
    * *Body d10:* 10 Slots.
    * *Mule Talent:* +4 Slots.
* **Overburdened:** If you exceed your limit, you gain the **Clumsy Tag** (-2 to Movement and Stealth) and cannot take the **Rush** action.

### THE FLAT SLOT RULE
**1 Item = 1 Slot.**
It does not matter if it is a knife, a rifle, or a med-kit. If it is significant enough to track, it takes 1 Slot.

* **Small Items:** "Pocket items" like keys, coins, a single lighter, or papers do not take slots.
* **Armor Restriction:** You may only carry **one suit of armor** at a time (worn). You cannot stack armor or carry spare suits in your pack — they are too bulky.

### GEAR CONDITION

Equipment degrades. The Director may impose a **Damaged** condition on a piece of gear when narratively appropriate — particularly after a Botch involving that item, a fall, or extended use without maintenance.

* **Damaged Gear:** -2 to any check using it. A firearm jams on any die result of 1 or 2.
* **Repairing Gear:** A **Tech Check (DR 12)** and access to a Repair Kit restores gear to working condition. Exceptional items (Relics, masterwork weapons) require a higher DR at the Director's discretion.
* **Destroyed Gear:** On a Botch involving a Damaged item, it may be permanently destroyed. The Director calls it.

## 4. WEAPONS

The tools of violence are varied, from rusted shivs to thunderous cannons.

* **Magical Weapons:** If a weapon is enchanted or forged of Old World steel, add **+1 to +3 Dice** of the same type to the damage roll (e.g., a +1 Magic Sword deals 3d6 damage).

### MELEE WEAPONS

| Weapon | Damage | Tags |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Unarmed** | 1d4 | Non-Lethal (unless Pugilist). |
| **Knife / Shiv** | 1d6 | Hidden, Thrown (Near). |
| **Club / Baton** | 1d8 | Non-Lethal option. |
| **Sword / Machete** | 2d6 | Reliable. |
| **Great-Axe / Sledge** | 3d6 | 2-Handed, Breaching (Destroys doors). |

### RANGED WEAPONS

| Weapon | Damage | Range | Tags |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pistol / Revolver** | 1d8 | Near | Quick Draw (Can fire in melee). |
| **Rifle / Musket** | 2d8 | Far | 2-Handed. |
| **Shotgun** | 2d8 | Near | Blast (Hits 2 targets in close proximity). |
| **Machine Gun** | 3d8 | Far | Heavy (Cannot move & fire), Auto-Fire. |
| **Cannon / Launcher** | 4d8 | Far | Heavy, Explosive (Zone Damage). |

## 5. ARMOR (THRESHOLD & PENETRATION)

Armor in *The Murk* is not about deflection; it is about absorption. However, every material has a breaking point.

### THE MECHANIC: THRESHOLD

Every suit of armor has a **Threshold** value.

* **Glancing Blow (Damage ≤ Threshold):** The armor holds. You take **Half Damage** (Round Up).
* **Penetration (Damage > Threshold):** The round punches through or finds a gap. You take **Full Damage**.

| Armor Type | Threshold | Penalty | Protection Context |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Clothing** | 0 | None | Prayer. Always takes Full Damage. |
| **Light** (Leather/Flak) | 6 | None | Reliably mitigates Knives (1d6, avg 3.5) and often Pistols (1d8, avg 4.5). Rifles (2d8, avg 9) usually penetrate. |
| **Heavy** (Plate/Hazmat) | 12 | Clumsy | Reliably mitigates Rifles (2d8) and Shotguns. Machine guns (3d8, avg 13.5) usually penetrate. |
| **Shield** | +1 Def | Hand | Adds to **Defense Rating** (Harder to hit), not Threshold. |

* **Note:** Heavy Armor applies the **Clumsy Tag** (-2 Move/Stealth) unless you possess the **Soldier** archetype.

## 6. AMMUNITION

Ammunition is tracked abstractly using **Ammo Packs**.

* **An Ammo Pack** represents a bandolier, pouch of shells, or loaded magazine — enough for **one Scene** of sustained combat use.
* **Running Dry:** If you roll a **Botch** on an attack roll, you are **Out of Ammo** until you spend an Action to reload with a new Pack.
* **Running Out Narratively:** The Director may rule that you've exhausted your Pack at the end of an extended firefight, especially if the scene dragged on. This is not a punishment — it's the cost of violence.
* **Conserving Ammo:** If you are trying to conserve, declare it before the scene. Your attack rolls take **-1**, but you do not consume the Pack unless you Botch.

## 7. GEAR & TOOLS

*Items that keep you alive. Each takes 1 Slot.*

* **Ammo Pack (Level 1):** A bandolier, pouch of shells, or a magazine. Contains enough shots for one Scene of intense combat. Botch on attack = Out of Ammo until reloaded.
* **Med-Kit (Level 2):** Alcohol, bandages, and adrenaline. Required to use the *Field Chirurgeon* talent or heal Stress via First Aid. (3 Uses).
* **Repair Kit (Level 2):** Tools, spare parts, and bonding agents for mechanical repair. Required to heal an Anthroparion's Stress or Suffering. Also needed to repair Damaged gear. (3 Uses).
* **Rations (Level 1):** Dried meat and clean water. Without this, you cannot Heal Stress during a Rest. Hunger is a slow killer.
* **Gas Mask (Level 2):** Essential in the deep Murk. Makes you immune to environmental **Lung Rot**. Filters last 1 Episode. Replacement filters are Level 1.
* **Lantern (Level 1):** Illuminates Near Range. Requires oil or batteries. The Murk hates light — this makes you a target.
* **Climbing Gear (Level 2):** Ropes, pitons, and crampons. Grants **+2** on Athletics: Climbing checks.
* **Lockpicks (Level 2):** Tension wrenches and rakes. Required to pick locks without breaking them.
* **Relic Compass (Level 3):** A twitching needle made of bone or brass. Points toward the nearest source of large-scale Magic or Murk activity. Also points toward Eidolons and Rogues.
* **Signal Flare (Level 1):** A chemical torch visible at Far Range through fog. Used to mark positions, call extraction, or trigger pre-arranged signals. Single use.
* **Toolkit (Level 1):** Wrenches, wire, and basic implements. Grants **+1d4** on Tech checks when working on mechanical devices (does not replace a Repair Kit).
* **Chalk & Candles (Level 1):** Required for Esoteric ritual work. A Ritual cast cannot begin without them unless the Esoterist has a Ritualist Talent modification.
* **Rope, 30ft (Level 1):** Load-bearing. Can support two adult humans. Essential for descents and extractions.

## 8. THE BLACK MARKET

Not everything can be Scrounged from ruins. Certain items move through back channels — the semi-criminal networks of Novemgrod's lower Rungs, the Gray Market under the Caranthian Quarter, the word-of-mouth fences who operate in plain sight because the Guard has decided to look elsewhere.

**Accessing the Black Market** requires either the **Friends in Low Places** Boon, a successful **Scrounge Check (DR 13)** in an urban area, or a **Streetwise** Ruffian.

**What the Black Market carries:** Specialist and Relic-tier items that would not be available through legitimate channels — unlicensed firearms, Esoteric components, information about Faction movements, forged papers, and occasionally something that was obviously looted from a place nobody should have been.

**The Markup:** Black Market prices are not negotiable in the standard sense. The vendor sets the terms and you either agree or leave. Attempting to haggle without leverage (reputation, threat, or something the vendor wants) simply ends the transaction.

**The Risk:** The Black Market is not a safe space. Every transaction is a potential sting, robbery, or introduction to someone's enemies. The Director may roll secretly to determine whether complications arise — and the Ruffian's **Streetwise** perk specifically neutralizes this ambiguity by establishing a relationship before the deal.
