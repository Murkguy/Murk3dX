# BOOK II: THE FATED (CHARACTER CREATION)

## CREATION STEPS AT A GLANCE

1.  **ASSIGN ATTRIBUTES:** Distribute **d10, d8, d6** to **Body, Mind, Spirit**.
2.  **CHOOSE ARCHETYPE:** Gain Unique Attribute (d8), Resource, and 3 Intrinsic Perks.
3.  **CHOOSE SPECIES:** Human, Murkborn, Anthroparion, or Chimera.
4.  **SELECT TALENTS:** Pick 1 Generic and 1 Archetype Talent. Take a **Burden** to gain a 2nd.
5.  **CHOOSE BOON:** Take 1 free Boon.
6.  **CALCULATE POOLS:** Injury Pool, Defense Rating, Load Slots.
7.  **GEAR:** Choose a Starter Package.

---

# CHAPTER 1: THE ATTRIBUTES

Your character is defined by three core Attributes. Each is assigned a die type—the larger the die, the more capable you are.

**At Character Creation, distribute the following dice: d10, d8, d6 — one to each Attribute.**

> "Assign your primary die where you expect to spend most of your time. A Soldier who never expects to talk their way out of trouble should put their d6 in Spirit."

---

## BODY
*Toughness, strength, physicality.*

Body governs everything that hurts you, everything you lift, and everything you endure.

* **Used for:** Melee attacks, physical saves (resisting poison, pain, exhaustion), Athletics, Physique, and Body-based combat shooting.
* **Determines:** How many Load Slots you carry (equal to die value) and contributes to your Defense Rating.
* **Save Dice:** When you take **Physical Stress** (bullets, blades, falls, poison), you roll **3 Body Dice** to resist it.

---

## MIND
*Intelligence, calculation, awareness.*

Mind governs your capacity to learn, deduce, and solve problems under pressure.

* **Used for:** Investigation, Lore, Medicine, Tech, Survival, and aimed combat shooting.
* **Determines:** How many Trained Skills you can hold (see Skills chapter).
* **Save Dice:** When you take **Mental Stress** (fear, magic backlash, psychological horror), you roll **3 Mind Dice** to resist it.

---

## SPIRIT
*Will, presence, belief.*

Spirit governs your force of personality, your connection to the divine, and your resistance to despair.

* **Used for:** Insight, Intimidation, Parley, Willpower, and resisting corruption.
* **Save Dice:** When you take **Spiritual Stress** (corruption, demoralization, soul damage), you roll **3 Spirit Dice** to resist it.

---

## THE FOURTH ATTRIBUTE: YOUR UNIQUE DIE
When you choose an **Archetype**, you gain a **Unique Attribute Die (d8)**: Tactics, Arcana, Devotion, or Guile. This die is used for Archetype-specific rolls and Initiative. It is always considered **Trained** and does not use a skill slot.

---

# CHAPTER 2: THE ARCHETYPES

Your Archetype is your survival strategy. It defines what you are good at, how your Resource works, and what advanced abilities you can unlock. All archetypes are designed to be useful at every point in the session—in the Murk, in the city, and in conversation.

Before you pick, understand how the Stress system works—every Archetype interacts with it.

---

## SURVIVING STRESS (THE BASELINE)
There is one **Stress Track**. Every source of damage—bullets, fear, magic—adds to this single number.

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**When you take damage:**
1. Add the damage to your Stress Track.
2. Immediately roll a **Save**: 3 Attribute Dice matching the damage type (Body, Mind, or Spirit).
3. **Success (Total > Current Stress):** You are fine.
4. **Failure (Total ≤ Current Stress):** Take a **Rank of Suffering**. Reset Stress to 0.

At **3 Ranks of Suffering**, you are **Broken**.

</div>

Each Archetype modifies this baseline—Soldiers have extra Body Dice, Esoterists have extra Mind Dice, and so on.

---

## THE FOUR ARCHETYPES

### [THE SOLDIER](01_Archetypes/SOLDIER.MD)
**The Specialist. The Veteran. The Wall.**

*You are the line in the sand. While others panic at the sight of a horror, you check your magazine and calculate the firing angle.*

* **Attributes:** Tactics (d8) + Body (d6)
* **Resource:** **Command Points (CP)** — Rolled from your Tactics Die at the start of each Combat scene. Expires at scene end.
* **Intrinsic Perks:**
  * **Standard Issue** — Ignore the Clumsy penalty on Heavy Armor and Heavy Weapons.
  * **Grit** — Roll 4 Body Dice on Physical Saves (instead of 3).
  * **The Bandolier** — Spend 1 CP to retroactively produce a combat consumable once per Scene.
* **Playstyle:** Tactical, frontline. High survivability, high action economy in combat. Weaker outside of combat unless talented into it.

---

### [THE ESOTERIST](01_Archetypes/ESOTERIST.md)
**The Researcher. The Occultist. The Volatile.**

*Magic is not a miracle; it is a science humanity has forgotten how to control. You understand that the laws of physics are suggestions, and with the right formula, you can rewrite them.*

* **Attributes:** Arcana (d8) + Mind (d6)
* **Resource:** **Agony** — Casting spells deals Stress to yourself (1d4 per Magnitude). You manage this by Venting (removing Stress while dealing nearby damage).
* **Intrinsic Perks:**
  * **The Sight** — Automatically detect active magic, curses, and Murk phenomena without rolling.
  * **Mental Fortress** — Roll 4 Mind Dice on Mental Saves against Agony (instead of 3).
  * **The Conduit** — Cast spells by taking 1d4 Stress per Magnitude. Spend an Action to Vent: remove 1d6 Stress (deals 2 damage to everyone in Near Range).
* **Playstyle:** High-power, high-risk. You can reshape any encounter, but mismanagement ends you. Your spellbook has three starting Principles; unlocking more is a talent investment.

---

### [THE ACOLYTE](01_Archetypes/ACOLYTE.md)
**The Shepherd. The Zealot. The Anchor.**

*You cannot throw lightning. But while others see a godless world, you see a design. You are the rock upon which your team stands.*

* **Attributes:** Spirit (d8) + Mind (d6)
* **Unique Attribute:** Devotion Die (d6) — Used for Petition rolls and Initiative. Steps up with XP.
* **Resource:** **Favor** — Rolled from your Devotion Die at the start of each session. Spent to petition your Patron for Miracles. Gained by fulfilling your Patron's Tenets; lost by violating them.
* **Intrinsic Perks:**
  * **The Covenant** — Only you can generate Favor and use the Petition system. Without you, the gods are silent.
  * **Shepherd of the Lost** — Free lodging at faithful institutions; +1d6 on social checks to de-escalate violence.
  * **Confessor** — Spend a Scene talking to an NPC; the Director reveals one Secret, Fear, or Regret they're hiding.
* **Playstyle:** Support and sustainability. You heal Stress, reveal information, and generate probabilistic miracles that give your group lucky breaks. Weaker in direct combat; essential in every other context.

---

### [THE RUFFIAN](01_Archetypes/RUFFIAN.md)
**The Scavenger. The Agent. The Shadow.**

*Honor is a luxury for the rich; you prefer survival. You fight dirty because there is no prize for dying fair.*

* **Attributes:** Guile (d8) + Agility (d6)
* **Resource:** **Trick Tokens** — Rolled from your Guile Die at episode start. Gained from Botches and optional Scene-start gambling. Spent to cheat fate, re-roll, and produce items from thin air.
* **Intrinsic Perks:**
  * **Streetwise** — Instantly locate Black Markets, Fences, or Safehouses in any settlement with a Guile check.
  * **Slippery** — Spend 1 Token to re-roll any failed Body or Agility Save (must keep the second result).
  * **Deep Pockets** — Spend 1 Token to produce a common utility item retroactively, once per Scene.
* **Playstyle:** Utility and mobility. You are the character who finds the way out, secures the loot, and ensures there is a tomorrow to worry about. You scale with clever play more than raw stats.

# CHAPTER 3: THE SPECIES (DIVERGENCES)
*[Full details: [Divergences](Divergences/DIVERGENCES.md)]*

In the years since the Calamity, humanity has branched. Some have remained unchanged. Others have been altered by the fog, the factories, or the old blood of the wild. At Character Creation, choose one.

---

### HUMAN
**The Desperate Majority.**

Humans lack the advantages of the altered kin, but they are relentlessly adaptable.

* **The Spark:** Gain **+1 to your Maximum Resource Cap** (Command Points, Agony, Favor, or Tokens).

---

### MURKBORN
**The Children of the Fog.**

Born inside the Murk's reach. Pale, hairless, with light-catching eyes. To them, the fog is home.

* **Fog-Lung:** Immune to Murk Toxicity and Lung Rot. You never roll Body Saves to breathe the fog.
* **Stigma:** +2 Difficulty on all Social checks with "Civilized" NPCs (Nobles, Merchants, Guards).

---

### ANTHROPARION
**The Souls in Iron.**

Human souls bound into bodies of ceramic, iron, and wood. They do not breathe or bleed, but they feel fear.

* **Plating:** Natural Armor 1. Reduce all Physical Stress by 1, minimum 0.
* **Inorganic:** Med-Kits have no effect. You require a Repair Kit and a Tech-trained ally to heal.

---

### CHIMERA
**The Wildtouched.**

Born of old pacts or magical fallout. Mostly human, but with distinct bestial traits.

* **Beast Blood:** Choose one mutation: *Predator* (Unarmed attacks deal d6), *Night-Eyes* (see in dim light), or *Thick Hide* (ignore extreme Cold or Heat).
* **Beacon:** Spirits, Murk-Entities, and supernatural enemies always prioritize you. You cannot hide from magical detection.

# CHAPTER 4: SELECTING TALENTS & BOONS
*[Full details: [Talents, Boons & Burdens](../13_Talents_Boons_and_Flaws/README.md)]*

---

## TALENTS
Talents are trained techniques and refined instincts. At Character Creation:

* **Pick 1 Generic Talent** (available to all Archetypes).
* **Pick 1 Archetype Talent** from your Archetype's Tier 1 tree.

**Generic Talents include:** Hard to Kill, Indomitable, Gunslinger, Field Chirurgeon, Danger Sense, Mule, Pugilist, Silver Tongue, Tinkerer, Light Sleeper.

> Archetype Talents are listed in each Archetype's full file. You can only take Tier 1 talents at character creation. Higher tiers are unlocked with XP.

---

## BOONS
Boons are advantages from your background—contacts, training, or natural gifts.

* **Take 1 free Boon** at character creation.
* **Take a Burden** (see below) to gain a 2nd Boon **or** 1 additional Generic Talent.

**Boon categories:** Social & Connections (Friends in Low Places, Noble Regalia, The Fixer, etc.), Survival & Grit (Iron Stomach, Jury Rigger, Scholar, etc.), Action & Combat (Die Hard, Dirty Fighting, Devil's Luck, etc.).

---

## BURDENS
Burdens are flaws, obligations, and curses that complicate your life.

* **Taking a Burden** gives you a 2nd Boon or a 3rd Talent.
* **When your Burden causes significant trouble** in a scene, you gain **1d6 Potential**. This is by design — your flaws generate momentum.
* You may only hold **1 Burden** at character creation.

**Burden categories:** Obligations & Curses (Vendetta, Debt, Haunted, Oathbound, etc.), Physiological & Mental Flaws (The Shakes, Glass Jaw, Illiterate, Hot Headed, etc.).

---

# CHAPTER 5: DERIVED STATISTICS

Once you've chosen your Archetype, Species, and Attributes, calculate these values.

---

## INJURY POOL
Your capacity to absorb punishment before something breaks.

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**Injury Pool = 3 × [Your Attribute Die value]**

*Which Attribute Die?* The die you assigned to the Attribute most relevant to your durability — typically Body for physical characters, Mind for mental specialists.

**Example:** Body d10 → Injury Pool = 30.

</div>

When your accumulated Stress approaches or exceeds your Injury Pool, Saves become increasingly difficult to pass.

---

## DEFENSE RATING (DR)
The difficulty attackers must beat to hit you.

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**Defense Rating = 10 + (Body Die Value ÷ 2)**

| Body Die | Defense Rating |
|:--|:--|
| d4 | 12 |
| d6 | 13 |
| d8 | 14 |
| d10 | 15 |
| d12 | 16 |

Armor adds to this (see Equipment).

</div>

---

## LOAD SLOTS (INVENTORY)
How much you can carry without becoming Overburdened.

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**Load Slots = Body Die value**

*Example:* Body d8 → 8 Load Slots.

One item = one slot (a knife and a rifle each take 1 slot).
Small items (keys, matches, papers) take no slots.

**Overburdened** (exceed Load Slots): -2 to Movement and Stealth; cannot Rush.

</div>

---

## STARTING SKILLS
*[Full details: [Skills](../12_Skills/README.md)]*

Your Mind Die determines how many Trained Skills you begin with.

| Mind Die | Trained Skills |
|:--|:--|
| d4 or d6 | 4 |
| d8 or d10 | 5 |
| d12 | 6 |

All characters begin with 0 Expert Skills. Mastery is earned through XP.
Your Unique Attribute (Tactics, Arcana, Devotion, Guile) is always considered Trained and does not use a slot.

# CHAPTER 6: STARTER GEAR PACKAGES

Every character begins with the basics: **3 Days of Rations, a Water Skin, and a Gas Mask.** Choose one of the following packages for the rest of your loadout.

---

### A. THE BREACHER
*For: Soldiers, anyone who expects to kick in doors.*

* Shotgun (2d6 Damage, Near Range, Blast — hits 2 adjacent targets)
* Machete (2d4 Damage, Reliable)
* Light Armor (Threshold 5)
* Ammo Pack (1 Scene of combat fire)

---

### B. THE SCOUT
*For: Ruffians, Soldiers who prefer range and mobility.*

* Rifle (2d6 Damage, Far Range, 2-Handed)
* Knife (1d4 Damage, Hidden, Thrown)
* Light Armor (Threshold 5)
* Climbing Gear (+2 to Athletics: Climbing)

---

### C. THE DUELIST
*For: Ruffians, anyone expecting close-quarters city work.*

* Revolver (1d6 Damage, Near Range, Quick Draw) **×2** — *if you have Gunslinger* — OR Revolver + Sword (2d4 Damage, Reliable)
* Heavy Coat (Light Armor, Threshold 5)
* Med-Kit (3 Uses)

---

### D. THE ARCANIST
*For: Esoterists only. Optimized for ritual work and fieldwork.*

* Pistol (1d6 Damage, Near Range)
* Relic Compass (Detects active magic and Murk phenomena in Near Range)
* Chalk & Candles (Required components for ritual casting)
* Ancient Book (Counts as a Lore tool; +2 to Lore checks for Old Empire topics)

---

### E. THE SHEPHERD
*For: Acolytes. Built around presence, healing, and endurance.*

* Revolver (1d6 Damage, Near Range)
* Ceremonial Staff (2d4 Damage, Reliable; doubles as a holy symbol)
* Light Armor (Threshold 5)
* Med-Kit (3 Uses)
* Incense & Reliquary (Required for Rite of Sanctification rituals)

---

> **A note on gear:** These packages represent what you had before this Episode began. You'll scrounge, trade, and steal everything else. Nothing on this sheet is permanent.
