# BOOK II: THE FATED (CHARACTER CREATION)

Before you take a single step into the Murk, you make a decision. Not about what weapon you're carrying or what faction you've offended. You decide who you are when everything goes wrong — because in this game, everything eventually goes wrong.

Character creation is not optimization. There is no perfect build. Every choice you make closes a door somewhere else and opens a different one. The Soldier survives what kills the Esoterist. The Ruffian escapes what traps the Acolyte. The question is not *which is best*. The question is *who do you want to be* in the moment the Murk closes in?

This chapter walks you through every decision, step by step. Read it once to understand the system. Then read it again with a character in mind.

---

## CREATION STEPS AT A GLANCE

1.  **ASSIGN ATTRIBUTES:** Distribute **d10, d8, d6** to **Body, Mind, Spirit**.
2.  **CHOOSE ARCHETYPE:** Gain Unique Attribute (d8), Resource, and 3 Intrinsic Perks.
3.  **CHOOSE SPECIES:** Human, Murkborn, Anthroparion, or Chimera.
4.  **SELECT TALENTS:** Pick 1 Generic and 1 Archetype Talent. Take a **Burden** to gain a 2nd.
5.  **CHOOSE BOON:** Take 1 free Boon.
6.  **CALCULATE POOLS:** Injury Pool, Defense Rating, Load Slots.
7.  **GEAR:** Choose a Starter Package.

This chapter walks through each step in full.

---

# CHAPTER 1: THE ATTRIBUTES

Three dice. Three questions about who you are when everything goes wrong.

Your character is defined by three core Attributes. Each is assigned a die type — the larger the die, the more capable you are under pressure. These dice are not just numbers. They are the skeleton of your character's identity. A character with a d10 in Spirit and a d6 in Body is fundamentally different from a character with those numbers reversed — not just mechanically, but narratively.

**At Character Creation, distribute the following dice: d10, d8, d6 — one to each Attribute.**

The d10 is your defining strength. The d8 is your secondary skill. The d6 is your vulnerability — the area where failure is most likely, and most interesting.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

*Vera is a former city-guard medic discharged after treating patients the city council had condemned. She expects to spend more time patching wounds than throwing punches.*

She assigns **Mind d10** (investigation, medicine, situational awareness), **Body d6** (she's capable, not a soldier), and **Spirit d8** (she's calm in crisis, persuasive when she needs to be). Her Archetype is Esoterist, which gives her **Arcana d8** — the die she'll roll whenever the equations matter.

Her weakest die — d6 Body — means she's fragile in a straight fight. That's a character decision, not a flaw.

</div>

> "Assign your primary die where you expect to spend most of your time. A Soldier who never expects to talk their way out of trouble should put their d6 in Spirit."

---

## BODY
*Toughness, strength, physicality.*

Body governs everything that hurts you, everything you lift, and everything you endure.

* **Used for:** Melee attacks, physical saves (resisting poison, pain, exhaustion), Athletics, Physique, and Body-based combat shooting.
* **Determines:** How many Load Slots you carry (equal to die value) and contributes to your Defense Rating.
* **Save Dice:** When you take **Physical Stress** (bullets, blades, falls, poison), you roll **3 Body Dice** to resist it.

---

## MIND
*Intelligence, calculation, awareness.*

Mind governs your capacity to learn, deduce, and solve problems under pressure.

* **Used for:** Investigation, Lore, Medicine, Tech, Survival, and aimed combat shooting.
* **Determines:** How many Trained Skills you can hold (see Skills chapter).
* **Save Dice:** When you take **Mental Stress** (fear, magic backlash, psychological horror), you roll **3 Mind Dice** to resist it.

---

## SPIRIT
*Will, presence, belief.*

Spirit governs your force of personality, your connection to the divine, and your resistance to despair.

* **Used for:** Insight, Intimidation, Parley, Willpower, and resisting corruption.
* **Save Dice:** When you take **Spiritual Stress** (corruption, demoralization, soul damage), you roll **3 Spirit Dice** to resist it.

---

## THE FOURTH ATTRIBUTE: YOUR UNIQUE DIE

When you choose an **Archetype**, you gain a **Unique Attribute Die (d8)**: Tactics, Arcana, Devotion, or Guile. This die is used for Archetype-specific rolls and Initiative. It is always considered **Trained** and does not use a skill slot.

Think of the Unique Die as the intersection of your training and your Archetype's identity. It is the die the Soldier rolls to seize initiative, the die the Esoterist rolls to cast — and the die that sets them apart from anyone else in the group who chose differently.

# CHAPTER 2: THE ARCHETYPES

Your Archetype is your survival strategy. Not just in combat — in every room, every conversation, every moment the Murk puts pressure on you. It defines what you are good at, how your Resource works, and what advanced abilities you can unlock.

Before you pick, understand how the Stress system works — every Archetype interacts with it differently.

---

## SURVIVING STRESS (THE BASELINE)

There is one **Stress Track**. Every source of damage — bullets, fear, magic — adds to this single number. There is no separate health pool and sanity meter. Pain is pain. The question is whether you absorb it or break under it.

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**When you take damage:**
1. Add the damage to your Stress Track.
2. Immediately roll a **Save**: 3 Attribute Dice matching the damage type (Body, Mind, or Spirit).
3. **Success (Total > Current Stress):** You are fine. Stress remains high — you are rattled, but standing.
4. **Failure (Total ≤ Current Stress):** Take a **Rank of Suffering**. Reset Stress to 0.

At **3 Ranks of Suffering**, you are **Broken**.

</div>

Each Archetype modifies this baseline. A Soldier rolls 4 Body Dice on Physical Saves instead of 3 — they absorb more punishment before they break. An Esoterist rolls 4 Mind Dice on Mental Saves — they can stare down horror that would shatter someone else's mind. These advantages are not small. Over the course of a session, an extra die on a Save is the difference between limping forward and dying on the floor.

---

## THE FOUR ARCHETYPES

### [THE SOLDIER](01_Archetypes/SOLDIER.MD)
**The Specialist. The Veteran. The Wall.**

*You are the line in the sand. While others panic at the sight of a horror, you check your magazine and calculate the firing angle.*

* **Attributes:** Tactics (d8) + Body (d6)
* **Resource:** **Command Points (CP)** — Rolled from your Tactics Die at the start of each Combat scene. Expires at scene end.
* **Intrinsic Perks:**
  * **Standard Issue** — Ignore the Clumsy penalty on Heavy Armor and Heavy Weapons.
  * **Grit** — Roll 4 Body Dice on Physical Saves (instead of 3).
  * **The Bandolier** — Spend 1 CP to retroactively produce a combat consumable once per Scene.

**How the Soldier works:** Command Points are an action economy resource, not just fuel. You spend them to give orders, activate maneuvers, and tip the scales at critical moments. The Bandolier means you never have to pre-plan your loadout perfectly — you can pull the flashbang you "should have grabbed" right when you need it. Grit means you absorb physical punishment better than anyone else at the table. The Soldier is not the character who deals the most damage. The Soldier is the character who is *still standing* when the damage is done.

* **Playstyle:** Tactical, frontline. High survivability, high action economy in combat. Weaker outside of combat unless talented into it.

---

### [THE ESOTERIST](01_Archetypes/ESOTERIST.md)
**The Researcher. The Occultist. The Volatile.**

*Magic is not a miracle; it is a science humanity has forgotten how to control. You understand that the laws of physics are suggestions, and with the right formula, you can rewrite them.*

* **Attributes:** Arcana (d8) + Mind (d6)
* **Resource:** **Agony** — Casting spells deals Stress to yourself (1d4 per Magnitude). You manage this by Venting (removing Stress while dealing nearby damage).
* **Intrinsic Perks:**
  * **The Sight** — Automatically detect active magic, curses, and Murk phenomena without rolling.
  * **Mental Fortress** — Roll 4 Mind Dice on Mental Saves against Agony (instead of 3).
  * **The Conduit** — Cast spells by taking 1d4 Stress per Magnitude. Spend an Action to Vent: remove 1d6 Stress (deals 2 damage to everyone in Near Range).

**How the Esoterist works:** The Agony system is a risk curve. A Magnitude 1 spell costs 1d4 Stress — manageable. A Magnitude 3 spell costs 3d4 — that is up to 12 Stress in a single action, which can trigger a Save at nearly any track level. The art of playing the Esoterist is knowing *when* to Vent. Venting is free Stress reduction, but it hurts your allies as much as your enemies. A Esoterist who Vents in a crowded room has a lot of explaining to do. The Esoterist's power ceiling is higher than any other Archetype. So is their body count when things go wrong.

* **Playstyle:** High-power, high-risk. You can reshape any encounter, but mismanagement ends you. Your spellbook has [three starting Principles](01_Archetypes/ESOTERIST/#the-spellbook-the-principles); unlocking more is a talent investment.

---

### [THE ACOLYTE](01_Archetypes/ACOLYTE.md)
**The Shepherd. The Zealot. The Anchor.**

*You cannot throw lightning. But while others see a godless world, you see a design. You are the rock upon which your team stands.*

* **Attributes:** Spirit (d8) + Mind (d6)
* **Unique Attribute:** Devotion Die (d6) — Used for Petition rolls and Initiative. Steps up with XP.
* **Resource:** **Favor** — Rolled from your Devotion Die at the start of each session. Spent to petition your Patron for Miracles. Gained by fulfilling your Patron's Tenets; lost by violating them.
* **Intrinsic Perks:**
  * **The Covenant** — Only you can generate Favor and use the Petition system. Without you, the gods are silent.
  * **Shepherd of the Lost** — Free lodging at faithful institutions; +1d6 on social checks to de-escalate violence.
  * **Confessor** — Spend a Scene talking to an NPC; the Director reveals one Secret, Fear, or Regret they're hiding.

**How the Acolyte works:** Favor is a moral resource, not a mana pool. You do not earn it by leveling up or spending XP. You earn it by *acting in accordance with your Patron's nature* — and you lose it by betraying it. This means your Devotion Die does not measure your power; it measures your *judgment*. A player who consistently embodies their Patron's values will roll more Favor dice on Petitions than someone who picks the Acolyte for the healing and ignores the theology. The Acolyte is the only Archetype that gets mechanically punished for playing against their concept. That is a feature, not a flaw.

* **Playstyle:** Support and sustainability. You heal Stress, reveal information, and generate probabilistic miracles that give your group lucky breaks. Weaker in direct combat; essential in every other context.

---

### [THE RUFFIAN](01_Archetypes/RUFFIAN.md)
**The Scavenger. The Agent. The Shadow.**

*Honor is a luxury for the rich; you prefer survival. You fight dirty because there is no prize for dying fair.*

* **Attributes:** Guile (d8) + Agility (d6)
* **Resource:** **Trick Tokens** — Rolled from your Guile Die at episode start. Gained from Botches and optional Scene-start gambling. Spent to cheat fate, re-roll, and produce items from thin air.
* **Intrinsic Perks:**
  * **Streetwise** — Instantly locate Black Markets, Fences, or Safehouses in any settlement with a Guile check.
  * **Slippery** — Spend 1 Token to re-roll any failed Body or Agility Save (must keep the second result).
  * **Deep Pockets** — Spend 1 Token to produce a common utility item retroactively, once per Scene.

**How the Ruffian works:** Trick Tokens are a chaos engine. Every other Archetype loses when they Botch. The Ruffian *gains* — a Botch hands you a Token. This creates a perverse incentive where bad luck is not something to dread but something to *almost enjoy*. A Ruffian who hits a string of bad rolls still has more options than one who plays it safe. Deep Pockets and Slippery mean you can survive situations that would hard-stop anyone else. The Ruffian scales with *clever play* more than raw stats. A mediocre Ruffian is a liability. A creative one is the reason the party is still alive.

* **Playstyle:** Utility and mobility. You are the character who finds the way out, secures the loot, and ensures there is a tomorrow to worry about.

# CHAPTER 3: THE SPECIES (DIVERGENCES)
*[Full details: [Divergences](Divergences/DIVERGENCES.md)]*

In the years since the Calamity, humanity has branched. Some have remained unchanged. Others have been altered by the fog, the factories, or the old blood of the wild. At Character Creation, choose one.

Your species is not a power level. It is a lens through which the world sees you — and through which you see the world.

---

### HUMAN
**The Desperate Majority.**

The Calamity did not change them. That is both their curse and their greatest weapon. Humans lack the instinctive advantages of the altered kin, but they are relentlessly adaptable — and in a world that keeps changing the rules, adaptability is the rarest currency.

* **The Spark:** Gain **+1 to your Maximum Resource Cap** (Command Points, Agony, Favor, or Tokens).

---

### MURKBORN
**The Children of the Fog.**

They were born inside the Murk's reach. Pale, hairless, with light-catching eyes — they are the proof that the fog is not only an ending, but a beginning. Other people call them cursed. The Murkborn call those people tourists.

* **Fog-Lung:** Immune to Murk Toxicity and Lung Rot. You never roll Body Saves to breathe the fog.
* **Stigma:** +2 Difficulty on all Social checks with "Civilized" NPCs (Nobles, Merchants, Guards).

The Murkborn is the rare species with a purely mechanical drawback in exchange for a purely mechanical advantage. The tradeoff is honest: you survive the environment that kills others, but you pay for it every time you walk into a room with money.

---

### ANTHROPARION
**The Souls in Iron.**

No one knows who built the first ones. The answer died in the Black Day, along with most of the records. What remains are the Anthroparions themselves — iron-framed, wood-jointed, ceramic-faced — walking proof that souls have no preference for flesh. They do not breathe or bleed. They still feel fear.

* **Plating:** Natural Armor 1. Reduce all Physical Stress by 1, minimum 0.
* **Inorganic:** Med-Kits have no effect. You require a Repair Kit and a Tech-trained ally to heal.

The Anthroparion trades party healing compatibility for baseline damage reduction. In a group with an Acolyte, the healing restriction matters less — Rite of Solace targets Mind and Spirit tracks, which Anthroparions take normally. In a group without one, having a Tech-trained ally becomes a logistical priority.

---

### CHIMERA
**The Wildtouched.**

Old pacts, wild blood, magical fallout. The cause doesn't matter. What matters is that something in you has answered a call that most people can't hear — and now the things in the dark hear it too. Chimeras are never invisible to the supernatural. They are also never powerless against it.

* **Beast Blood:** Choose one mutation: *Predator* (Unarmed attacks deal d6), *Night-Eyes* (see in dim light), or *Thick Hide* (ignore extreme Cold or Heat).
* **Beacon:** Spirits, Murk-Entities, and supernatural enemies always prioritize you. You cannot hide from magical detection.

The Chimera's Beacon drawback is not cosmetic. In missions involving the supernatural — which is most of them — enemies will target you first. Factor this into your combat positioning. A Chimera Soldier is a dangerous deterrent that *wants* to be targeted. A Chimera Ruffian needs to think carefully about stealth.

# CHAPTER 4: SELECTING TALENTS & BOONS
*[Full details: [Talents, Boons & Burdens](../13_Talents_Boons_and_Flaws/README.md)]*

---

## TALENTS

Talents are trained techniques and refined instincts — the things you have learned to do that the average person cannot. At Character Creation:

* **Pick 1 Generic Talent** (available to all Archetypes).
* **Pick 1 Archetype Talent** from your Archetype's Tier 1 tree.

**Generic Talents include:** Hard to Kill, Indomitable, Gunslinger, Field Chirurgeon, Danger Sense, Mule, Pugilist, Silver Tongue, Tinkerer, Light Sleeper.

> Archetype Talents are listed in each Archetype's full file. You can only take Tier 1 talents at character creation. Higher tiers are unlocked with XP.

---

## BOONS

Boons are advantages from your background — contacts, training, or natural gifts that existed before the campaign started.

* **Take 1 free Boon** at character creation.
* **Take a Burden** (see below) to gain a 2nd Boon **or** 1 additional Generic Talent.

**Boon categories:** Social & Connections (Friends in Low Places, Noble Regalia, The Fixer, etc.), Survival & Grit (Iron Stomach, Jury Rigger, Scholar, etc.), Action & Combat (Die Hard, Dirty Fighting, Devil's Luck, etc.).

---

## BURDENS

A Burden is not a penalty. It is a fuel source.

Every time your Burden causes significant trouble in a scene — not minor inconvenience, but genuine, story-complicating trouble — you earn **1d6 Potential**. The game is designed to make your flaws matter, because flaws that never surface are just unread fine print. A character with a Burden and a good Director is a character who generates momentum.

* **Taking a Burden** gives you a 2nd Boon or a 3rd Talent.
* You may only hold **1 Burden** at character creation.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

*Cassius has the **Hot Headed** Burden. He is a Soldier — disciplined in the field, volatile in conversation. Midway through a tense negotiation with a Black Market fence, the Director describes the fence making a dismissive comment about Cassius's dead squad.*

*The Director calls it: this is the Burden triggering. Cassius's player can lean in — Cassius snaps, the negotiation collapses, the party has to fight their way out instead of paying their way through. Consequence: 1d6 Potential earned. The Potential funds the desperate sprint to the extraction point that follows. His flaw paid for the escape.*

</div>

**Burden categories:** Obligations & Curses (Vendetta, Debt, Haunted, Oathbound, etc.), Physiological & Mental Flaws (The Shakes, Glass Jaw, Illiterate, Hot Headed, etc.).

# CHAPTER 5: DERIVED STATISTICS

Once you've chosen your Archetype, Species, and Attributes, calculate these values. These numbers do not change with every session — they are the baseline you work from.

---

## INJURY POOL
Your capacity to absorb punishment before something breaks.

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**Injury Pool = 3 × [Your Attribute Die value]**

*Which Attribute Die?* The die you assigned to the Attribute most relevant to your durability — typically Body for physical characters, Mind for mental specialists.

**Example:** Body d10 → Injury Pool = 30.

</div>

When your accumulated Stress approaches or exceeds your Injury Pool, Saves become increasingly difficult to pass.

---

## DEFENSE RATING (DR)
The difficulty attackers must beat to hit you.

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**Defense Rating = 10 + (Body Die Value ÷ 2)**

| Body Die | Defense Rating |
|:--|:--|
| d4 | 12 |
| d6 | 13 |
| d8 | 14 |
| d10 | 15 |
| d12 | 16 |

Armor adds to this (see Equipment).

</div>

---

## LOAD SLOTS (INVENTORY)
How much you can carry without becoming Overburdened.

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**Load Slots = Body Die value**

*Example:* Body d8 → 8 Load Slots.

One item = one slot (a knife and a rifle each take 1 slot).
Small items (keys, matches, papers) take no slots.

**Overburdened** (exceed Load Slots): -2 to Movement and Stealth; cannot Rush.

</div>

---

## STARTING SKILLS
*[Full details: [Skills](../12_Skills/README.md)]*

Your Mind Die determines how many Trained Skills you begin with.

| Mind Die | Trained Skills |
|:--|:--|
| d4 or d6 | 4 |
| d8 or d10 | 5 |
| d12 | 6 |

All characters begin with 0 Expert Skills. Mastery is earned through XP.
Your Unique Attribute (Tactics, Arcana, Devotion, Guile) is always considered Trained and does not use a slot.

---

## PUTTING IT TOGETHER: A WORKED EXAMPLE

The numbers are easier to follow with a character in front of you.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

**Vera. Esoterist. Human.**
Attributes: Body d6 / Mind d8 / Spirit d6 / Arcana d8.

- **Injury Pool:** Mind is her durability anchor. 3 × 8 = **24**
- **Defense Rating:** 10 + (6 ÷ 2) = **13**
- **Load Slots:** 6 (Body d6 value)
- **Starting Skills:** 5 (Mind d8)
- **Starting Stress Max:** 10
- **Resource:** Agony (Human Spark gives her cap of 11)

At DR 13, she is not hard to hit. With an Injury Pool of 24 and Mental Fortress (4 Mind Dice on Mental Saves), she can absorb magical backlash better than almost anyone — but a stray bullet at the wrong moment will end her. She takes The Arcanist gear package and picks **Ritualist** as her Archetype Talent at Tier 1: utility rituals for zero Stress, as long as she has a minute to work.

</div>

# CHAPTER 6: STARTER GEAR PACKAGES

Every character begins with the basics: **3 Days of Rations, a Water Skin, and a Gas Mask.** Choose one of the following packages for the rest of your loadout.

These packages represent what you had before this Episode began. You'll scrounge, trade, and steal everything else. Nothing on this sheet is permanent.

---

### A. THE BREACHER
*For: Soldiers, anyone who expects to kick in doors.*

You don't look for a way around. You make one.

* Shotgun (2d6 Damage, Near Range, Blast — hits 2 adjacent targets)
* Machete (2d4 Damage, Reliable)
* Light Armor (Threshold 5)
* Ammo Pack (1 Scene of combat fire)

---

### B. THE SCOUT
*For: Ruffians, Soldiers who prefer range and mobility.*

Distance is survival. You learned that early.

* Rifle (2d6 Damage, Far Range, 2-Handed)
* Knife (1d4 Damage, Hidden, Thrown)
* Light Armor (Threshold 5)
* Climbing Gear (+2 to Athletics: Climbing)

---

### C. THE DUELIST
*For: Ruffians, anyone expecting close-quarters city work.*

The city is your terrain. You know its rhythms.

* Revolver (1d6 Damage, Near Range, Quick Draw) **×2** — *if you have Gunslinger* — OR Revolver + Sword (2d4 Damage, Reliable)
* Heavy Coat (Light Armor, Threshold 5)
* Med-Kit (3 Uses)

---

### D. THE ARCANIST
*For: Esoterists only. Optimized for ritual work and fieldwork.*

The gun is a last resort. Your real weapon is the geometry in your head.

* Pistol (1d6 Damage, Near Range)
* Relic Compass (Detects active magic and Murk phenomena in Near Range)
* Chalk & Candles (Required components for ritual casting)
* Ancient Book (Counts as a Lore tool; +2 to Lore checks for Old Empire topics)

---

### E. THE SHEPHERD
*For: Acolytes. Built around presence, healing, and endurance.*

You go where the flock goes. Even into the dark.

* Revolver (1d6 Damage, Near Range)
* Ceremonial Staff (2d4 Damage, Reliable; doubles as a holy symbol)
* Light Armor (Threshold 5)
* Med-Kit (3 Uses)
* Incense & Reliquary (Required for Rite of Sanctification rituals)
