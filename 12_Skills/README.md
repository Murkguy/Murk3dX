# CHAPTER 5: SKILLS

In *The Murk*, competence is the difference between breathing and rotting. Your Attributes define your raw potential, but your Skills define your training. Two characters with the same Mind Die are not equally capable — the one who has studied locks opens doors, and the one who hasn't breaks their picks.

---

## 1. THE SKILL TIERS

Every skill is rated in one of three Tiers. This applies a flat modifier to your Result.

### UNTRAINED (Penalty: -2)
*The Novice. You have seen it done, but you have never done it under fire.*
* **The Mechanic:** `2d6 + Attribute Die - 2`
* **Impact:** You are clumsy and hesitant. Standard Tasks (DR 10) are not guaranteed. A bad die roll means humiliating failure on something a professional would do in their sleep.

### TRAINED (Bonus: +2)
*The Professional. You have done this for a living. You know the protocols.*
* **The Mechanic:** `2d6 + Attribute Die + 2`
* **Impact:** Standard Tasks (DR 10) are nearly automatic. Hard Tasks (DR 15) are achievable. Impossible Tasks (DR 20) require luck on top of training.

### EXPERT (Bonus: +4)
*The Master. You have written books on the subject, or you are simply a savant.*
* **The Mechanic:** `2d6 + Attribute Die + 4`
* **Impact:** Hard Tasks (DR 15) are routine. Impossible Tasks (DR 20) are within reach on most rolls.

---

## 2. STARTING SKILLS (THE MIND LIMIT)

Your capacity to learn and retain complex training is determined by your **MIND DIE**.

**At Character Creation:**
* **Mind d4 or d6:** Choose **4** Trained Skills.
* **Mind d8 or d10:** Choose **5** Trained Skills.
* **Mind d12:** Choose **6** Trained Skills.

* All characters start with **0 Expert Skills**. Expert rating is earned through XP (8 XP to advance one Skill from Trained to Expert).
* Your Unique Attribute (Tactics, Arcana, Devotion, Guile) is always considered **Trained** and does not use a slot.
* Skills not in your list are Untrained. You can still attempt them — you just do it badly.

---

## 3. DIFFICULTY RATINGS

The Director sets the DR before the roll, not after. Knowing what you're up against before you commit is part of the game.

| DR | Label | What it means |
|:--|:--|:--|
| **8** | Easy | Something a trained professional barely needs to think about. Reserve for dramatic tension, not gatekeeping. |
| **10** | Standard | Competent performance under pressure. A trained character should succeed most of the time. |
| **13** | Elevated | A meaningful challenge. Untrained characters will fail more often than not. |
| **15** | Hard | Requires training *and* luck. Even experts have off days. |
| **18** | Severe | The margin for error is essentially zero. |
| **20** | Impossible | Beyond normal human capability without exceptional circumstances. |

**Modifiers the Director may apply:**
* **Excellent Tools or Conditions:** -2 to DR
* **Poor Tools or Conditions:** +2 to DR
* **Time Pressure (rushed):** +2 to DR
* **Time to Prepare:** -2 to DR

---

## 4. CONTESTED CHECKS

When two characters directly oppose each other — a chase, a lie vs. Insight, a grapple — both roll and compare totals. The higher result wins. Ties go to the initiating character (they had the initiative).

*Example:* A Ruffian tries to pickpocket a Merchant. The Ruffian rolls Stealth, the Director rolls the Merchant's Insight. The higher result determines the outcome.

---

## 5. COMBINED SKILL CHECKS

Sometimes two skills apply simultaneously. The Director may call for a **Combined Check** — roll both applicable skills and use the *lower* result. This represents the constraint: you are only as good as your weakest link.

*Example:* Scaling a crumbling wall while under fire — Athletics to climb, Willpower to keep moving while taking hits. Roll both, use the worse result.

Alternatively, for tasks where one skill supports another, the Director may grant **+1d4** to the primary check if the supporting skill is Trained or Expert.

## 6. THE SKILL LIST

### COMBAT SKILLS (BODY / MIND)
*Violence requires tools. Being good with a knife does not make you good with a rifle.*

* **BRAWL (Body):** Unarmed fighting, grappling, tackles, and choke holds.
* **MELEE (Body):** Hand-held weapons — knives, swords, axes, clubs.
* **SMALL ARMS (Body/Mind):** Handguns, revolvers, SMGs. See §7 below.
* **LONG GUNS (Body/Mind):** Rifles, shotguns, muskets. See §7 below.
* **HEAVY WEAPONS (Body):** Machine guns, cannons. Requires a stable firing position.

### PHYSICAL SKILLS (BODY)
* **ATHLETICS:** Climbing, swimming, jumping, falling, and balancing under pressure.
* **PILOT:** Operating vehicles — trucks, steamwalkers, boats — in dangerous or complex conditions. Routine driving requires no roll.
* **STEALTH:** Moving silently, hiding in cover, tailing a target without being noticed.
* **PHYSIQUE:** Raw strength — forcing a jammed door, holding a structure open, pinning someone down.

### MENTAL SKILLS (MIND)
* **INVESTIGATION:** Searching environments for clues, evidence, or hidden objects. Also used for detailed looting.
* **LORE:** Recalling history, geography, monster identification, Old Empire technology, and theological doctrine.
* **MEDICINE:** Diagnosing injury and disease, field surgery, stabilizing a dying character, and interpreting autopsy results.
* **SCROUNGE:** Finding, sourcing, and acquiring equipment outside of normal channels — through ruins, black markets, nervous traders, and forgotten caches. See §8 below.
* **SURVIVAL:** Tracking, hunting, foraging, navigation, and estimating how long something has been dead.
* **TECH:** Repairing machines, picking mechanical locks, hotwiring engines, and understanding Old Empire schematics.

### SOCIAL SKILLS (SPIRIT)
* **INSIGHT:** Reading body language, detecting deception, and sensing emotional states.
* **INTIMIDATION:** Compelling compliance through threat, physical presence, or reputation.
* **PARLEY:** Persuasion, diplomacy, negotiation, formal deception, and barter.
* **WILLPOWER:** Resisting psychological pressure — torture, manipulation, prolonged stress, and mental endurance.

---

## 7. USING MIND IN COMBAT

When using **Small Arms** or **Long Guns**, the player must describe *how* they are shooting to determine the Attribute.

* **BODY (Reflex):** Snap-shooting, suppressing fire, shooting while moving, or panic-firing.
* **MIND (Calculation):** Sniping, ambushing, or taking a carefully aimed shot from a stable position.

The distinction matters. A Soldier with a high Body but low Mind is excellent in close-quarters scrambles. A character with high Mind but low Body should not be trying to shoot while sprinting.

---

## 8. SCROUNGE (FINDING EQUIPMENT)

The Murk does not have a store. Equipment comes from ruins, traders, scavengers, and people with less leverage than you.

**Scrounge** is the skill for finding, sourcing, and acquiring gear outside of normal channels. It represents a combination of street knowledge, salvage instinct, and the ability to find the right person in a desperate city.

### THE ROLL
Roll `2d6 + MIND + Scrounge Bonus` and compare to the DR for what you're looking for.

| DR | Tier | Examples |
|:--|:--|:--|
| **10** | Common | Food, clean water, basic ammo, bandages, alcohol, rope, matches. |
| **15** | Specialist | Working firearms, Med-Kits, fuel, batteries, gas mask filters, climbing gear, lockpicks. |
| **20** | Relic | Old Empire technology, magical components, heavy weapons, masterwork armor, Esoteric instruments. |

### THE RISK
Scrounge takes time and exposure. You are in someone else's territory, asking questions about things that might get you killed.

* **Failure:** You find nothing useful. Time was spent. You may try again next Scene, but the Director may rule that the well is dry in this location.
* **Botch (Fail + natural 1 on Attribute Die):** You find trouble — a sting operation, a hostile fence, a cursed item, or a competitor who has been watching the same cache.

### SCROUNGE MODIFIERS
* **Urban (Novemgrod, active markets):** No modifier.
* **Settlement (small outpost, frontier town):** +2 DR.
* **Ruins (looting directly):** +4 DR but no Barter cost — what you find, you keep.
* **Having a contact (Friends in Low Places boon):** -2 DR.
* **Time to search (1 full Scene):** -2 DR.

### BARTER
Finding someone who has the item is half the work. Acquiring it costs a Barter transaction — trading something of equal or greater Value Level. See [Economy & Gear](/09_Equipment/) for the full Barter system.
