# THE MISTCLIFFS OF NOVEMGROD

**Novemgrod** is the westernmost bastion of the Eastern Shelf, the great uprising of land that was thrust skyward during the Calamity. It stands as a lonely sentinel, surrounded by the Murk on the West and the endless, scorched silence of the Arid Waste to the East.

Many quarters of the lower city were swallowed by the endless mist decades ago, leaving only the highest peaks of the Shelf habitable. Only through the Herculean efforts of the Builder's Guilds did the city survive at all, as men and women masoned for weeks without sleep to shore up the crumbling foundations against the dissolving tides of the fog.

### A PATCHWORK SKYLINE
What remains is a landscape of absurdist architecture. Desperate to house refugees from the fallen lowlands, the city built up. Ramshackle tenements cling like barnacles to the few remaining ancient spires of the Old Imperium. It is a city of monumental impression met with determined desperation—gold-leafed statues staring down into muddy, coal-choked alleyways.

When the Murktide shifts, warning bells ring throughout the districts. Teams of workers—the "**Shore-Men**"—move quickly to reinforce the foundations of the westernmost plazas before the mist returns to claim them.

---

## THE PALATINE REGIUM
Dominating the skyline is the legendary **Palatine Regium**, a fortress-citadel dating back to the Old Imperium of millennia ago. It is the heart of the city, not just politically, but physically.

However, the Regium is not merely a castle; it is an anomaly.

* **The Resonance:** As one approaches the massive, iron-shod gates of the citadel, a sound begins to manifest. It is not heard with the ears, but felt in the teeth and the marrow of the bone—a low, rhythmic thrumming, like the heartbeat of a sleeping titan. The closer one gets to the Keep, the louder the resonance becomes. It is this invisible vibration that seems to physically repulse the heaviest banks of the Murk, creating a bubble of breathable air around the upper city.
* **The Silence:** The Regium is guarded by a specific regiment known as **The Null Watch**. They wear heavy, lead-lined greatcoats and fully enclosed helms of polished brass with no visible eye-slits. They never speak. They never remove their helms in public. They stand perfectly still for hours, only moving with terrifying speed to bar entry to the unauthorized.

The Principate of the City, **Alexei Novikov**, has forbidden the searching of the Regium’s lower levels. He claims the Resonance is dangerous to the uninitiated mind.

## THE GILDED CAGE (WHY WE STAY)
Outsiders often ask why the populace does not simply flee East, away from the encroaching fog. The answer is grim and absolute: *There is nowhere to go.*

### THE BARRIER OF ASH
To the East lies the **Arid Waste**. The weapons used in the Great War did not just destroy the enemy; they murdered the soil itself. The Waste is a thousand miles of glassed earth and salt storms. No water flows there. To flee into the Waste is a slow, agonizing death by thirst. To stay in Novemgrod is to gamble with madness, but at least there is water.

### THE PULSE
The populace stays because of the **Resonance**. Whatever is buried beneath the Palatine Regium is the only thing keeping the air breathable. The hum is a constant reminder of their precarious survival. If the thrumming were to stop, even for an hour, the Murk would rush in and drown the city. The people are tethered to the sound like divers to an air hose.

### THE LURE OF THE DEEP
There is also Greed. The Murk destroys life, but it preserves matter. The Sunken Districts beneath the fog are frozen in time, filled with the wealth of the Old World: gold, art, and invaluable **Archeotech**.

* **The Economy of Salvage:** Novemgrod is the only place on Tellus where a pauper can become a Prince in a single afternoon by finding a working Fusion Core. The danger is absolute, but the payout is infinite. This has given rise to the **Divers**—the brave and the foolish who descend into the dark to keep the city’s economy alive.

## THE PRINCE AND THE MAGISTER

### PRINCE ALEXEI NOVIKOV (Nobles, Bereft of Nobility)
Prince Alexei Novikov was never meant to be Grand Prince. His uncle, Milov, despised him for much of his life. "You are too soft," Milov would sneer. "You know nothing of the world, only of books."

Constant comparisons were made to Alexei's cousin, Rikov, a prince of great prowess in battle and two years his senior. Rikov was handsome, athletic, and beloved by the people. While he and Alexei were friends, Rikov never understood the bookish, sickly Alexei. Bound to be an administrator for life, Alexei retreated into philosophy, content to be a shadow.

**The Phantom War:** Then, The War began. Milov and Rikov left Novemgrod in Alexei's hands. They marched West with their armies and their Battle-Magisters to face the encroaching dark. They never came back. The mist took them.

Alexei inherited the shattered remains of his home. He had to rebuild in the shadow of ghosts.

* **The Ruler:** Alexei is a wise ruler for a man so young, an "Enlightened Despot." He disregards the corrupt will of the Noble Councils in favor of the plight of his subjects. Yet, he is paranoid. He trusts no one. A palace coup is always brewing, and though he looks frail, within his gaze lies an otherworldly insight that unsettles his generals.

### HIGH MAGISTER VALESKA (The Unspoken Alliance)
Alexei frequently seeks out **High Magister Valeska** when in need of council. Their meetings are polite and friendly, a sharp contrast to the hostility between the Church and the Academy.

* **The Bond:** The Magister needs him for political survival; he needs her to interpret the fluctuations of the Resonance. Something deeper connects them—they are kindred spirits, the "Smart Ones" left behind by the "Strong Ones" to clean up the mess. But the station of their roles forbids any union. They are married to the City, not each other.

## THE DISTRICTS (THE VERTICAL CITY)

Novemgrod does not sprawl. It climbs. The city is built in strata — elevation is status, and to fall is literal.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

The street vendors say: "God lives at the top. The fog lives at the bottom. And the rest of us live in between."

They are not wrong.

</div>

### THE CROWN (TOP TIER)
The highest inhabited district, perpetually above the worst of the Murk. The air is cold and clear.

* **The Palatine Regium** dominates the Crown — its towers visible from every district. The Null Watch patrols a four-block exclusion zone around the outer walls.
* **Noble Townhouses** cluster on the Crown's eastern face, their facades still bearing the carved stone crests of Houses that lost half their members to the Phantom War. Many are maintained by skeleton staffs; some are quietly occupied by Schola affiliates who bought the deeds from desperate heirs.
* **The Schola Magisterium Headquarters** occupies a converted fortress on the northern Crown. Its interior is a labyrinth of locked archives, experimental chambers, and offices staffed by people who write down everything and say nothing. The public facade is an open library that everyone knows is not actually open to the public.
* **The Cathedral Quarter** houses three competing faiths within four city blocks: the Makru Orthodox High Temple (stone, enormous, deliberate), the Penitent Choir Central Hall (plain, crowded, loud), and a small Xelas observatory tower that maintains a polite silence about the other two.

### THE RUNGS (MIDDLE TIER)
Most of Novemgrod lives here. The Rungs are dense, warm from the factory heat rising below, and saturated with the accumulated noise of a hundred thousand people trying to survive.

* **The Market Streets** of the Second Rung operate seven days a week. You can buy almost anything here, from Suomi furs to Caranthian engine parts, and the vendors are trained to not notice where things came from.
* **The Guildhalls** occupy the solid, pre-war buildings along the First Rung's central avenue. The Builder's Guild Hall is the largest. The Divers' Guild Hall is the most heavily guarded.
* **The Black Market Quarter** does not have a name on any official map, but everyone in the Rungs knows the two-block stretch behind the Third Rung tanneries. The Ruffian's **Streetwise** perk represents knowing which storefront is actually a front.
* **The Staging Grounds:** At the western edge of the Rungs, before the city's edge drops into the Fogline, are the Diver staging areas — warehouses full of equipment, debrief rooms, and the medical bays that see more traffic than the Crown's hospital.

### THE BOILER (LOWER TIER)
Below the First Rung, above the Fogline. Hot, dark, loud. The factories run here day and night, and the smoke mixes with the Murk fog on bad days into something that makes breathing a considered decision.

* **The Factories** produce the metalwork and munitions that keep Novemgrod defended. The conditions are brutal. The Builder's Guild controls the labor contracts. The workers know this and hate it and have nowhere else to go.
* **The Slum Tenements** pack the Boiler's side streets. Murkborn families cluster here by necessity — they can breathe where the air quality drives surface-dwellers back up. Refugee populations from the Lost Lands occupy the eastern Boiler blocks; the Dorian refugee quarter has been here for two generations and at this point is simply a neighborhood.
* **The Crime Syndicates** operate openly in the Boiler. Prince Alexei knows about them. He has decided that enforcing Boiler law fully would cost more guards than he has. The syndicates do not push into the Rungs. This is an unofficial arrangement.

### THE FOGLINE (CONTESTED TERRITORY)
Not a district — a moving boundary. The lowest inhabited streets, where some buildings are half-swallowed. Condemned above, occupied below.

* **The Shore-Men** work here constantly, shoring up foundations, hauling salvage up from the Sunken Districts, and maintaining the boundary markers that tell residents how far the fog crept overnight.
* **Squatter Communities** occupy the Fogline buildings that still have intact upper floors. The city does not officially acknowledge they exist. The Shore-Men bring them food occasionally. No one talks about it.
* **Entry Points:** The major Diver access shafts descend from the Fogline. A Diver on their way down walks through streets that are half fog, half stone, with the hum of the Resonance louder here than anywhere in the city, rattling loose glass and setting teeth on edge.

## THE POWER STRUCTURE (WHO ACTUALLY RUNS THE CITY)

Novemgrod has many authorities. None of them fully controls it.

### PRINCE ALEXEI (Nominal Sovereign)
Alexei holds the throne, signs the writs, and commands the Null Watch. In a crisis, his word is law. Outside a crisis, he governs through persuasion and paranoia in roughly equal measure. His intelligence is not in question. His ability to translate that intelligence into durable institutional control is. He is thirty-one years old and has aged visibly since the Phantom War.

### HIGH MAGISTER VALESKA (The Schola Magisterium)
Valeska controls the most comprehensive intelligence network in the city — not because she built one, but because the Schola's scholars study everything and write it down. She knows what the Resonance is. She has told Alexei a partial version of this. She has not told him the rest, because what she knows cannot be un-known, and she is not certain he would remain functional afterward.

### THE NOBLE COUNCILS (The Korovladi and Their Kin)
The old families still hold their council seats, still vote on taxation and trade policy, still show up to Crown events in their war-worn finery. Their actual power has been quietly bled away over two decades of Alexei's careful administrative maneuvers. They know this. They are funding three separate coup attempts and hoping one takes. The Korovladi family is the most prominent and the most dangerous — they hold the salvage licensing monopoly for the northern descent shafts.

### THE GUILDS (The Real Economy)
The Builder's Guild controls construction, repair, and the labor contracts that keep the Boiler running. The Divers' Guild controls who goes into the fog and what comes back. Between them, they govern more of daily life in Novemgrod than the Prince's office does. Guild Masters do not attend Crown functions. They host their own events and quietly note who comes.

### THE CHURCH (Contested)
Both the Makru Orthodox and the Penitent Choir operate in Novemgrod, and both want the Crown's formal endorsement. The Orthodox already has the institutional infrastructure — courts, schools, the Builder's Guild tithe. The Choir has the people; half the Boiler goes to their Sunday meetings. Alexei has made no formal declaration of preference. Both factions interpret his silence as opportunity.

## THE DIVERS (THE ECONOMY OF SALVAGE)

No Diver thinks they are going to die. That is the requirement of the job.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

The briefing room smells like oil and old fear. The Guild rep spreads the descent map across the table without ceremony — a patchwork of hand-drawn notes, crossed-out landmarks, red marks where someone didn't come back.

"Third block east, the fog shifted last week. Don't trust anything past the old bank building." She taps one red mark specifically. "We don't know what that is. Don't find out."

She rolls the map up. "Standard split. Sixty to you, thirty to the Guild, ten to the recovery fund in case we have to come and get what's left of you." She hands you a brass tag with a number on it. "Wear this. We'll use it to identify the body if we need to."

She says it without cruelty. It's just the terms.

</div>

### DIVER RANKS
The Divers' Guild tracks experience with a ranking system that serves both as status and as a warning to employers about what they're actually buying.

| Rank | Title | What It Means |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | Greenleg | First descent. Still has the look of someone who thinks it will be fine. |
| **2** | Fogwalker | Survived three descents. Knows where the common dangers are. Stops flinching at the Resonance hum. |
| **3** | Deepwalker | Veteran. Has been to the Sunken Districts proper. Has left someone down there. |
| **4** | Ghost | A Deepwalker who keeps coming back when others don't. The name is a euphemism. |

Rank is displayed on the brass Guild tags that Divers wear on descent. A Greenleg with a Ghost's tag is a liar. The Ghost will let the Guild know.

### THE SUNKEN DISTRICTS
The fog preserved the old city below the Fogline in near-perfect condition — which means it also preserved everything that was in it when the Murk came.

* **What's Down There:** Pre-Calamity architecture. Old Imperium infrastructure. Archeotech: power cells, communication arrays, medical devices, weapons technology. Libraries. Vaults. The personal effects of a hundred thousand people who did not make it to higher ground.
* **The Dangers:** The Murk breeds things in the dark. Daemons nest in the Sunken Districts. The fog distorts navigation, turns familiar streets into mazes. Certain buildings are structurally compromised; the wrong step collapses a floor into three more floors. And occasionally, what Divers find down there was left there deliberately, by someone who didn't want it found.
* **The Eidolon Problem:** Deepwalkers know that the Sunken Districts have at least two Eidolons in permanent residence. One is mapped — a massive, slow-moving entity that has claimed the Old Imperium administrative block as territory. The other has never been seen clearly enough to map. Divers call it "the Understudy." They go around the mapped one. They stay nervous about the other.

### THE DIVER'S CODE
Unwritten. Absolute.

1. **You carry your partner out.** If they're dead, you carry the body. If you can't carry the body, you mark the location and the Guild sends a recovery team. You do not leave them unmarked.
2. **You don't jump another Diver's claim.** The descent map shows who's working which sector. If you're in the same sector, you split. If you find their cache before they do, you tag it with their number and walk away.
3. **You surface when you said you'd surface.** The Shore-Men run on schedules. If you're late, they send a team down. That team is risking their lives because of your bad planning. You owe them.
4. **You don't sell what you find to the Korovladi.** They set the market price low and then resell at a markup. The Guild has negotiated rate floors with every other major buyer in the city. The Korovladi monopoly is the one thing Divers and Guilds are in complete agreement about.

## WHAT NOVEMGROD WANTS FROM YOU

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**ADVENTURE HOOKS**

1. **The Resonance Fluctuation.** Three nights ago, the Resonance hum dropped in pitch for roughly forty minutes, then resumed. The Murk advanced twelve meters into the First Rung during the gap before retreating. No announcement from the Palatine Regium. No statement from Valeska's office. The Shore-Men who were on duty that night are now officially "on leave." Someone in the Rungs is quietly paying to find out what happened, and what it means if it happens again.

2. **The Korovladi Problem.** A Diver named Oskar Fen found something in the Sunken Districts last week — didn't say what, but was seen entering the Korovladi townhouse on the Crown the next morning. He hasn't been seen since. Oskar's Guild tag was dropped off at the Divers' Guild intake window in an envelope. No note. His sister is asking around.

3. **The Missing Schola Project.** A Schola Magisterium research team of four was assigned to a descent into Sector 9 — the old university district — two weeks ago. They were supposed to surface after six hours. The Schola has quietly filed a missing persons report with the Crown rather than investigating internally, which means they don't want it to be Schola business officially. The reason for that is unclear and interesting.

4. **The Choir vs. the Order.** A Makru Order of the Hammer investigator has arrived in the Boiler district, claiming to be tracking a Charred One cell. The Penitent Choir's local leadership is refusing him access to their congregation records, which the investigator says contain two names. The Prince's office has declined to compel the Choir to cooperate. The investigator is now asking for "private assistance." The factory that burned down last month is in the Boiler. Nobody died. But the Choir's community kitchen was in that factory.

## THE ARCHITECTURE (THE FOLDED CITY)

When the Calamity struck, the Shelf did not simply rise. It folded.

The tectonic violence that thrust the Shelf skyward did so unevenly — some sections tilted, some buckled, some rose faster than their neighbors. Buildings that had stood for centuries were not torn down by this upheaval. They were pushed. Towers that once stood vertical now lean at angles of fifteen, twenty, thirty degrees. Facades that faced west now face the sky. Streets that ran level now slope steeply enough to require iron grip-rails bolted into the cobblestones.

Rather than demolish what stood, Novemgrod's builders did what they always do: adapted.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

The first thing visitors notice is that nothing is straight.

Not the streets — they curve and tilt and occasionally pass *under* another street via stone arches that were never intended to be arches. Not the buildings — they lean toward each other over the narrow alleys, some nearly touching at their upper floors, connected now by rope bridges, enclosed walkways, iron catwalks bolted to facades as afterthoughts. Not the skyline — it is a jagged, impossible thing, onion domes beside baroque clock towers beside ancient Imperial spires, none of them plumb.

The second thing visitors notice is that the city is tied together. Literally. Steel cables, iron chains, and thick rope-and-timber frameworks run between buildings at every level, the whole mass of the upper city bound together like cargo that might slide off a moving cart. Because it might. Because it has, sometimes. Because the Shelf is still not entirely done moving.

</div>

The **Cable Districts** — those sections of the city held together by the Builder's Guild's post-Calamity engineering — are Novemgrod's structural backbone. The cables are maintained constantly. When a cable snaps, the buildings it connects shift. Sometimes this is harmless. Sometimes a wall cracks. Twice in recorded history, a cable failure set off a cascade that brought down an entire block. The Builder's Guild employs full-time cable-walkers who inspect the network section by section, every day, without exception.

The result is a city that feels organic in a way that cities usually don't — grown rather than planned, accumulated rather than designed. Every neighborhood has its own texture, its own angle of lean, its own relationship to gravity. Visitors who have been in the city long enough stop noticing. Visitors who have just arrived find it profoundly unsettling, like standing on a ship in permanent, motionless swell.

## THE CATHEDRALS

Novemgrod has more houses of worship than hospitals, more competing faiths than competing guilds, and a Prince who has declined to endorse any of them. The result is a theological free market in which architecture is theological argument made permanent.

### THE HIGH TEMPLE OF MAKRU (THE IRON CATHEDRAL)

The largest structure in the Cathedral Quarter and the oldest building on the Crown still in continuous use. Built of grey stone in the Imperial style — massive, deliberately severe, with no decorative element that does not serve a structural purpose. The keystone of every arch bears the Hammer. The floor is polished black marble quarried from pre-Calamity deposits.

The Iron Cathedral is where the Korovladi families hold their official ceremonies, where guild contracts are notarized before God, and where every Prince of Novemgrod has been formally recognized. Whether Alexei will submit to formal recognition — which requires swearing an oath to Makru's temporal authority — is an open question he has been avoiding for six years.

### THE CENTRAL HALL OF THE PENITENT CHOIR (THE HOUSE OF SILENCE)

Not a cathedral in the architectural sense — it was a concert hall before the Calamity, and its identity has never fully resolved. The Hall has high ceilings built for acoustics, rows of dark wooden pews salvaged from three different demolished chapels, and a stage where the Choir's singers once performed liturgical music. They no longer sing here. Since the Black Day, the Hall is used for the charitable work of the Choir's mainstream congregation, and the stage is where Brother Cassin delivers his sermons.

The original Le Vides chapel — a small, plain room at the back of the Hall — is kept locked. The Choir says it is a place of private contemplation. The lock is new. Nobody asks what changed.

### THE CATHEDRAL OF LE VIDES (THE SILENT CHURCH)

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

The doors have not opened in thirty-two years.

They are iron, two inches thick, set into a stone arch that was carved when this was the most important building in the lower city. The carvings are still beautiful — singers, hands raised, mouths open. Stopped mid-note. The iron is not rusted. Someone maintains it. Nobody admits to going near it.

At the right time of morning, when the wind comes from the west and the Resonance is low, you can stand close to the doors and hear something from inside. It is not singing. It is not silence, either. It is something that exists in the space between.

</div>

The Cathedral of Le Vides stands in the Rungs, tilted eight degrees from vertical by the Calamity, its bell tower now leaning toward the street at an angle that required three separate cable braces to prevent it from falling. The cathedral was the spiritual center of the city before the Black Day. On the morning of the Calamity, the Choir inside stopped singing mid-verse and never started again.

The Flagellant wing of the Penitent Choir has begun holding vigils outside the doors on the anniversary of the Black Day. Sister Marek says the church is not abandoned — Le Vides is still inside, waiting. The Choir's mainstream congregation does not engage with this claim publicly. The iron doors have not opened. They are not visibly locked from the outside. Nobody has tried to open them. This fact is not discussed.

### THE XELAS OBSERVATORY

A converted clock tower on the northern face of the Crown, four stories taller than anything around it due to the Calamity's tilt working in its favor. The Xelas fellowship operates the city's only functioning astronomical instruments from the top floor. They share their star charts with anyone who asks and maintain a reading room on the second floor that is genuinely open to the public, unlike the Schola's "library."

The observatory tower leans south by eleven degrees. The Xelas priests have installed a system of counterweights that keeps the telescope platform level regardless. They consider this an elegant solution. The Builder's Guild considers it structurally inadvisable and has said so in writing three times.

## THE CLIFFSIDES

The westernmost edge of the city, where the Shelf drops in a series of ragged terraces down toward the Fogline and the Murk beyond. Elevation here is measured not in status but in survival probability.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

You smell the Cliffsides before you see them. Coal smoke and wet stone and something underneath both of those — organic, damp, the smell of things that live in dark wet places. The gas lamps here are spaced further apart than in the Rungs. Some have been dark for years. Nobody has come to fix them.

The buildings are not so much built as accumulated. A pre-Calamity warehouse became a tenement became a twelve-family dwelling with walls added between load-bearing columns and floors split into half-floors when the ceiling height allowed. People live in spaces that were never meant to be lived in. They have done this for thirty years and they are not leaving.

At night, if you stand near the western edge and the Resonance is low and the wind is still, you can hear the fog. It doesn't make a sound, exactly. It makes the absence of a sound — a silence that has weight and direction.

</div>

The Cliffsides is Novemgrod's oldest inhabited neighborhood and its most dangerous. When the Shelf rose and the lower city was swallowed by the Murk, the population that survived and couldn't afford to move higher settled here — on the terraces, in the leaning pre-Calamity buildings, in structures that the Builder's Guild has officially condemned and the city guard officially does not patrol after dark.

**What happens here:**
- The Shore-Men stage out of the Cliffsides. Their equipment warehouses, the decompression rooms (for those who spend too long breathing filtered air at fog-level), and the communal kitchens that feed the crews are all here. The Shore-Men are the Cliffsides' most organized residents, and this provides a thin membrane of institutional presence.
- The Murk creeps closer at night. Cliffsides residents keep their own fog bells — hand-rung, neighborhood-maintained, independent of the city's official warning system. Every family has a gas mask within arm's reach.
- The things that come out of the fog when the tide is low come up through the Cliffsides first. The Shore-Men deal with this. Sometimes they don't deal with it in time.
- There are streets in the western Cliffsides that the Shore-Men have written off — too close, too compromised, the buildings half-swallowed and occupied by things that are not Shore-Men. The city has no official record of what lives there now. The Cliffsides residents have unofficial records. They do not share them with outsiders.
- The **Hollows Market** operates in a condemned warehouse on the second terrace: the Cliffsides' economy in miniature, running on barter and desperation, selling things salvaged from the near-fog that the Divers' Guild does not officially acknowledge finding.

## THE FOUNDRIES

The industrial district of the Boiler has eleven foundry complexes. Four are operational. Three are partially operational — producing at reduced capacity with compromised equipment and skeleton crews. Four are silent.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

Foundry Six went quiet on a Tuesday. No announcement — the smoke just stopped. The workers had been there the Friday before. By Monday they were gone. The Guild Master's office said "production relocation." Nobody moved anything out. The equipment is still inside.

Nobody goes into Foundry Six.

</div>

The operational foundries produce the iron and steel that the Builder's Guild needs for cable maintenance, structural reinforcement, and the slow, ongoing project of keeping the city's buildings from completing their tilt into the streets below. They run day and night, and the workers who run them live in the Boiler's tenements and have the characteristic cough — from the coal smoke, from the metal dust, from the occasional Murk-seep that gets into a building's lower levels before anyone notices.

The silent foundries are a different problem. Some were abandoned due to equipment failure. Some lost their worker populations to the depopulation of the past decade. Some stopped for reasons the Guild Master's office has not disclosed. They sit in the Boiler like gaps in a set of teeth — dark buildings in rows of lit ones, doors chained, windows dark, the equipment inside either salvaged or left exactly as it was when the last shift walked out.

Several have been occupied. By whom varies: Charred cultists treat the furnace rooms as holy sites. Looter bands use the loading bays as staging grounds. One foundry — Foundry Nine — has been quietly taken over by a community of Murkborn families who have bricked up the windows and run their own heating from salvaged parts. The Builder's Guild knows about Foundry Nine. They have not moved on it because the Murkborn maintain the building, which is more than the Guild does.

## THE NORTHERN RUINS

More than half of Novemgrod's pre-Calamity population is gone. Some died in the war. Some fled east. Some are simply missing — the kind of missing that happens when a neighborhood is swallowed slowly, over years, and the people in it stopped leaving records.

The result is a northern section of the city that is nominally still Novemgrod — still within the Resonance's protective radius, still above the Murk — but functionally abandoned. The buildings stand. Many are structurally sound. The streets are recognizable. Nobody lives there.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

The orphanage on Voronka Street still has the children's names written on the doors of the dormitory rooms, in chalk, because chalk lasts longer than the people who wrote it. Whoever left last closed the doors and locked the gate. The lock is still locked. The gate is slightly open — the post it's attached to has shifted two inches in thirty years of Calamity-settling. Inside, in the main hall, the tables are set for a meal that was never served.

</div>

**What is in the Northern Ruins:**

The city's abandoned institutional buildings — schools, orphanages, civic halls, a half-finished hospital — stand exactly as they were left, filled with records, furniture, medical equipment, and everything else too heavy or too irrelevant to evacuate. For salvagers, this is a trove. For the things that moved in after the people left, it is territory.

The Northern Ruins are not ungoverned — they are governed badly, by whoever is currently strongest in a given block. These change:

* **Charred Cultist Warrens:** Two or three cells have established semi-permanent presence in the northern ruins, using the abandoned buildings as congregation points. They do not hold territory in the organized sense; they move between buildings, maintaining caches of materials and occasionally conducting the kind of activity that produces fire damage on buildings that weren't on fire before.
* **The Hollow Court:** The largest single faction in the ruins — a loose confederation of deserters, criminals, and people who chose disappearance over whatever Novemgrod was offering them. They are not unified by ideology but by the practical arrangement of controlling four adjacent city blocks well enough that nobody comes in without permission. Their leader is a former Dorian Army sergeant named Voss who handles disputes with a functional officer's court martial process that is somehow more just than anything available in the Rungs.
* **Cannibal Warrens (Eastern Block):** The eastern edge of the ruins. Nobody talks about it directly. The Shore-Men won't go there. The Divers who have scouted the area looking for Undercroft access points describe evidence of organized habitation with a diet that is not sustainable from foraging alone. The city guard has no plan to address this. The city guard does not go into the Northern Ruins.
* **Rogue Mercenary Holdings:** Three or four small companies — too small to get legitimate contracts, too dangerous to ignore — have claimed individual buildings as bases. They do not cooperate with each other and occasionally fight over supply routes. They extract protection money from the Hollow Court (which pays, grudgingly) and from the occasional civilian who still lives in the ruins' southern fringe.

The Resonance reaches the Northern Ruins — the air is breathable, the fog stays back — but the city's other services do not. No gas lamps. No water mains (most have been broken for a decade). No telegraph lines. No postal service. No watch. You are on your own.

## THE UNDERCROFT

Beneath the Shelf, running through the rock itself, is the tunnel network the city calls **the Undercroft** — miles of carved passages following the veins of a mineral that the mining guilds officially call *Mirestone* and that everyone else calls **Murk-coal**.

Mirestone is ordinary rock that has been saturated, over decades, by Murk seeping up through fissures in the Shelf. The saturation process changes the stone's properties: it becomes faintly phosphorescent (dim blue-green, enough to navigate by in complete darkness), combustible under specific industrial processes, and capable of generating sustained heat output far beyond ordinary coal. It also becomes slightly radioactive in a manner that the Schola Magisterium has been studying for fifteen years and has not publicly characterized.

The miners who work the Undercroft are called **Seam-workers**. A medical condition associated with extended Mirestone exposure — a progressive lung and neurological deterioration — is called **Seam-sickness**. The mining guild's official position is that Seam-sickness is caused by inadequate ventilation, not by the stone itself. The Schola's position is that the data is inconclusive. The Seam-workers' position is that everyone who works long enough gets it eventually.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

The glow is the thing you notice first. Not light, exactly — the Mirestone doesn't illuminate, it just *isn't quite dark*. Your lamp is still necessary. But the walls have a quality to them, a faint cold luminescence that makes the tunnel feel like the inside of something living.

The veteran Seam-worker ahead of you has not spoken since you entered. She navigates by markings scratched into the walls — not official markings, personal ones, accumulated over years. She knows where the seam runs and where it thins. She knows which passages have been sealed and why.

She stops at a junction and holds up her hand. You hear it: a low, distant groan, the sound of settling stone or something else, you can't tell. She listens for thirty seconds. Then she continues.

You ask her later what she was listening for. She says: "The difference."

She doesn't explain what the difference is between.

</div>

**The Undercroft's Problems:**

* **Murk Breakthrough:** Periodically — without predictability or warning — a section of tunnel breaks through to a Murk pocket below the Shelf. The Murk floods in. The standard protocol is immediate evacuation and tunnel sealing. The protocol works, roughly, when applied in time. When it isn't applied in time, the section is sealed with whoever is inside still inside. The sealed sections are marked on official maps. There are also unmarked ones.
* **The Changed:** Seam-workers who have been in the Undercroft for years sometimes change in ways that go beyond Seam-sickness. Their skin takes on a faint blue-grey pallor. They become more comfortable in the dark than in the light. They start to hear things in the tunnel sounds that others don't hear. The mining guild's official term for this is "overexposure syndrome." The unofficial term is simply "the Changed." The Changed are not removed from duty; they are too experienced and the guild needs them. They are assigned to the deepest sections, away from newer workers.
* **What Else Lives Down There:** The Undercroft goes deeper than the official maps show. The sealed Murk-breakthrough sections are on the maps. The passages beyond them are not. Seam-workers who have explored beyond the seals — and some have; the Changed find them easier to navigate — describe architecture that predates the Shelf. Pre-Calamity. Possibly pre-Imperial. Nobody has authorized an expedition. The Schola has applied for access permits twice. Both applications are pending.

## THE OLD CITY

At the base of the Cliff — below the Cliffsides, below the Shore-Men's territory, below the point where the fog retreats fully — the original Novemgrod still stands.

The Old City predates the Calamity by centuries. It was a prosperous trading city, the westernmost port of the old trade routes, built in a style that shows the influences of three different empires: the rounded domes of the Ruthenian interior, the severe stone arcades of Dorian civil architecture, the ornate minarets of Turcian traders who settled the merchant quarter. It was a beautiful city. The fog came up around it thirty-two years ago and it has been under fog ever since.

Unlike the deep Sunken Districts, which were swallowed quickly and catastrophically, the Old City went under slowly — the fog rose block by block over years as the Murk's edge advanced. The buildings were not destroyed. They were preserved. The fog keeps out moisture, decay, insects, and time. Inside the Old City's buildings, as far as anyone who has been inside and returned can report, everything is exactly as it was.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

The Diver who came back from the Old City — the third one, the one who survived — described the market square. The stalls still had goods on them. Not rotted. Not collapsed. Simply there, exactly as they were left. A bolt of fabric in a color she had never seen before, perfectly preserved. A child's shoe, single, in the middle of the square with no explanation. The fountain in the center still worked — the water pressure from some mechanism below still running after three decades of fog.

She stayed for forty minutes. She said it was the most peaceful place she had ever been.

She said she would not go back.

</div>

**What makes the Old City different from the standard Sunken Districts:**

* The buildings are structurally intact and navigable without the demolition hazards of the collapsed lowland ruins.
* The Murk concentration is lower here — the Old City sits in a bowl created by the Shelf's rise, and the Murk flows around it at higher concentration than inside it. Divers report that gas mask filters last roughly twice as long as in equivalent-depth Sunken Districts.
* The preservation is total. Whatever is in the Old City is in its original condition. This includes the goods in the market stalls, the books in the library, the contents of the Grand Archive building, the personal effects in every house, the treasury of the old merchant council — and whatever was in the Old City's cathedral, the doors of which are sealed with a lock that no Diver's tools have opened.
* Something is in the Old City. Not the ambient Murk fauna — the Murk-rats, the Fog Walkers. Something that belongs there, that has been there since before the fog came. Divers who have spent extended time in the Old City report the sense of being observed. Two have reported direct encounters — one described it as "a person standing very still who was not a person." Neither encounter resulted in violence. Both resulted in the Diver surfacing immediately and refusing to return.
* The Eidolon documented in the Esoteria records as "the Resident" is believed to have its origin in the Old City. It ranges into the Sunken Districts but returns. Nobody knows why.

</div>
