## 2. CARANTHA (THE CHAOS OF FREEDOM)
**Status:** Active / Unstable Democracy.

Situated at the farthest reaches of the Eastern Shelf, the archipelago of Carantha is a land of jagged peaks and smoking vents. Historically, it was the Empire's mailed fist—a culture of unwavering martial tradition that valued discipline above civil liberty. Their spires were not adorned with saints, but with carvings of drills, marching columns, and triumphant executions.

### THE RIVALRY
Following the collapse of the Old Empire, Carantha emerged as the primary rival to Doria. For centuries, they waged a bloody war over the borderlands of Ruthenia, turning the fields into a charnel house of steel and mud.

### THE DAY THE KINGS DIED
The Black Day saved Carantha by destroying its ambition. The fateful Battle of Doria (0 AC) saw the gathered might of the Caranthian Royal House annihilated by the initial surge of the Murk. The entire VarZolen Dynasty was wiped out, save for one nephew, Rorn, who inherited a kingdom of corpses.
Rorn's reign lasted days. He was cut down by an assassin’s blade, ending the royal line forever.

### THE NEW EXPERIMENT
Seizing the vacuum, activists and visionaries drove the nation toward a terrifying new era: Democracy. Over the past three decades, Carantha has transformed into a Confederation of City-States.
* **The Reality:** It is not a utopia. It is a mess. Political factions operate like street gangs, vying for influence. The loss of their expert military leadership has left them vulnerable, but their spirit is unbroken. They are free, and they will kill anyone who tries to take that away.

### THE CARANTHIAN SPIRIT
To play a Caranthian is to be loud, proud, and armed.

**1. BY SEA AND AIR**
Carantha is an archipelago of high-altitude peaks that rise above the Murk like islands in a black ocean.
* **The Sky-Captains:** Caranthians are the undisputed masters of the air. They pilot majestic Iron-Clad Dirigibles and gas-haulers that soar between the mountain peaks, ferrying goods and soldiers over the fog.
* **The Aesthetic:** A Caranthian doesn't walk; they swagger. They wear aviator leathers, heavy coats, and goggles as status symbols.

**2. THE GREAT FORGE**
Nestled at the base of a dormant volcano lies the industrial heart of the nation.
* **The Industry:** The intense geothermal heat allows Carantha to excel in metallurgy. While Novemgrod scavenges for Old Tech, Carantha builds new steel.
* **The Craft:** Their weapons are heavy, brutal, and reliable. A Dorian sword is a piece of art; a Caranthian hammer is a tool for murder.

**3. AGGRESSIVE HOSPITALITY**
One of the hallmarks of their culture is their legendary, almost competitive hospitality.
* **The Tradition:** It is said that no one leaves a Caranthian home without being fed to bursting. This is not just kindness; it is a display of dominance. To feed a guest is to prove you are strong enough to spare resources.
* **In Play:** A Caranthian NPC will buy the whole bar a round of drinks, then punch you in the face if you refuse to drink it.

**4. THE CITY-STATE CONFEDERATION**
Carantha is not one nation; it is a collection of rival cities (e.g., Iron-Port, High-Reach, Sulfur-Gate).
* **The Conflict:** Each city has its own laws. In one, dueling is legal; in another, it is a capital crime. Navigating Carantha requires knowing which faction owns the ground you stand on.

## KEY FACTIONS

* **The Iron-Port Syndicate** controls Caranthian arms exports and is currently squeezing the Aviator's Brotherhood out of the northern trade routes by bribing city-state customs officials to impose route fees the Brotherhood can't absorb.
* **The Aviator's Brotherhood** is the union of Sky-Captains and crew — fiercely independent, largely ungovernable, and the only organization in Carantha capable of coordinated action across all the city-states simultaneously, which is why the Syndicate is scared of them.
* **The Democratic Reform Council** is the faction that actually believes in the democracy they founded, and they are losing. They are the minority voice on three of the five major city-state councils, and they know that the Syndicate is three good elections away from buying the whole confederation.

---

## WHAT BRINGS YOU HERE

Carantha is the only place on the Shelf where you can buy heavy weapons and ask questions later. The Great Forge produces the machinery the rest of the Near Lands need, and the city-state confederation means there's no single government to say no to anything. If you need an airship, a cannon, a contact who knows the northern fog channels, or passage above the Murk to somewhere that isn't reachable on foot, you come to Carantha.

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

1. A Caranthian arms shipment bound for Novemgrod has gone missing mid-route — the dirigible arrived, the cargo manifest matches, but three crates of heavy weaponry are unaccounted for. The Iron-Port Syndicate wants this resolved quietly, before the Aviator's Brotherhood can use it as leverage in the current route dispute. They're paying well. They're also possibly responsible.

2. The Democratic Reform Council has three days to produce a witness who can testify to Syndicate bribery before a city-state council vote that will effectively hand Carantha's trade policy to the Syndicate permanently. The witness exists. They are in Sulfur-Gate, a city where the Syndicate currently has two sitting council members and a very cooperative city guard.

3. The volcano vent beneath Iron-Port has been unstable for six weeks. The city's engineering guild says it's fine. An independent geothermal surveyor who was hired to confirm this has disappeared, and her notes were found floating in the harbor. The Forge owners have an obvious financial interest in this being fine. It may not be fine.
