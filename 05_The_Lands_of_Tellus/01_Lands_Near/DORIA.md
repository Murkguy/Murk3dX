## 1. DORIA (THE LOST HEART)
**Status:** Fallen / Consumed by Murk.

Set deep within the heart of the old Vardan Empire, the lands that would later become the country of Doria hold an illustrious history. This region served as the birthplace of the original sixteen emperors, a testament to its significance as the pinnacle of power on all of Tellus. Indeed, the entirety of Doria was intricately intertwined with the legacy of the Vardan Empire, sharing not only its territories but also a capital city that remained unchanged—Varranthal.

### THE RISE AND FALL
The zenith of Doria's influence began to wane following the ascension of Emperor Regieran to the realm beyond in 1331 BBD. In the wake of his departure, the empire descended into a tumultuous era of internal strife.

Yet, amid the political turmoil, Doria experienced a resurgence under the reign of King Arminus Magnus (745 BBD). With remarkable acumen, Arminus consolidated the fragmented political landscape, ushering in a new golden era. Doria reclaimed much of its former homeland and even extended southward, subjugating the land of Kemet.

### THE BLACK DAY (0 AC)
However, the tides of fate took an irreversible turn with the advent of The Black Day. As the catastrophic event unfolded, Doria found itself at the epicenter. Its lands were consumed by the insidious Murk—a rolling sea of malevolent black fog that devoured all in its path.

The once-thriving nation became a desolate wasteland. Varranthal, the capital city that once symbolized the might of the Vardan Empire, now lies silent and lifeless beneath the fog—a ghostly reminder of the nation's former grandeur.

### THE DORIAN EXILE (CULTURE & PEOPLE)
*The Nation in Refuge.*

Only the expatriate Dorians managed to escape the grim fate, seeking refuge in nearby lands like Kemet and Novemgrod. Despite their scattered settlements, they steadfastly cling to their customs, carrying the flame of Dorian heritage wherever they go.

To play a Dorian (or interact with one) is to understand four pillars of their society:

**1. THE HOUSES OF LEGACY**
Within the remnants of the Empire, the noble houses serve as the bedrock of political influence. Many individuals take great pride in tracing their ancestry back to the Vardan Empire.
* **The Obsession:** They meticulously maintain extensive genealogical records. These ancestral connections are seen as a source of honor and legitimacy. A Dorian Noble in a refugee slum will still demand to be addressed by their full title.

**2. THE MATRIARCHY**
Dorian society is distinctly Matriarchal.
* **The Matrons:** Women hold significant influence, assuming the role of decision-makers and arrangers of marriages. Matrons play a central role in selecting suitable matches for their children, considering factors such as social standing and strategic alliances.
* **In Play:** If you want a favor from a Dorian House, you do not talk to the Duke; you talk to his Mother.

**3. HONORABLE DUELS**
The concept of personal honor is paramount. Conflicts or disputes are often resolved through ritualistic combat.
* **The Code:** These duels are not brawls; they are strictly regulated affairs used to settle grievances or restore honor. To refuse a duel is to accept social death.

**4. THE GUILD SYSTEM**
In the fabric of Doria's society, an intricate guild system thrives.
* **The Structure:** Each guild operates as a self-governing body. They include the Guild of Arcanists, Guild of Blacksmiths, Guild of Traders, and Guild of Scribes.
* **The Power:** Membership offers social recognition and access to exclusive networks. Even in exile, a Dorian Blacksmith will only teach their secrets to a fellow Guild member.

**5. THE OCCUPATION (Dorian Military Abroad)**
The Dorian army did not die with the nation. The military command structure survived the Black Day intact — without a homeland to defend, it became something else: an occupying force in search of territory to justify its existence.

* **Kemet:** The Dorian Army's largest surviving formation has occupied the northern territories of Kemet for the past thirty years, extracting resources under the justification of "restoring order to the region." Kemet had order before they arrived. Kemet does not say this out loud.
* **The Soldier's Paradox:** A Dorian soldier is intensely proud of their nation's history and deeply uncomfortable with what the army is currently doing in its name. Most handle this by not thinking about it. A few have deserted. The deserters are not welcomed back.

---

Doria is a ghost that refuses to stop walking. The nation is gone — consumed, swallowed, erased — and the people carry it with them anyway, in their titles and their guild seals and their genealogy ledgers. Somewhere under the Murk, the capital city of Varranthal still stands, perfectly preserved and unreachable. Some Dorian nobles fund Diver expeditions to locate its position. None have succeeded. It is possible the city is still occupied.

## KEY FACTIONS

* **The Dorian Noble Council in Exile** meets monthly in a rented hall in Novemgrod's First Rung to vote on legislation for a nation that no longer exists, funding themselves through the salvage rights to the Sunken Districts that were once Dorian territory, which the Korovladi are currently contesting.
* **The Dorian Army's Southern Command** occupies northern Kemet, extracts resources, and sends a percentage back to fund the Noble Council — an arrangement that benefits both parties and costs only the Kemetan people.
* **The Guild of Scribes** is the only Dorian guild that has genuinely thrived post-exile, because their product (information, contracts, records, forgeries) is weightless and portable. Every city with a significant Dorian population has a Guild of Scribes chapter. Their forgeries are excellent. Their records are even better.

## WHAT BRINGS YOU HERE

"Here" is complicated — Doria no longer exists as territory. To travel among the Dorian diaspora is to move through refugee communities in Novemgrod, occupation zones in Kemet, and the occasional fortified estate where a noble family has refused to accept that their land title refers to something under the fog. Dorian work tends to involve questions of legitimacy: who really owns what, who actually has the right they're claiming, and what records would prove or disprove either.

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

1. The Dorian Noble Council has commissioned an expedition to locate the position of Varranthal under the Murk — not to enter it, officially, but to confirm coordinates for a future reclamation effort. The expedition needs Divers. The Korovladi have been informed of this plan and are offering twice the Council's rate to anyone who will report what the expedition finds, because a legitimate Dorian claim to the Sunken Districts would invalidate the salvage licensing monopoly they've built their finances on.

2. A Guild of Scribes chapter in Novemgrod is holding a set of genealogical records that a Korovladi family has paid to have destroyed — the records prove a Dorian noble family's superior claim to an estate currently occupied by the Korovladi. The Scribes are refusing to destroy the records on principle. The Korovladi have stopped asking politely.

3. A Dorian Army deserter from the Kemet occupation has arrived in Novemgrod with evidence of a massacre — not a battle, a massacre — in a Kemetan village. He wants the evidence given to someone who can act on it. The problem is that "someone" does not obviously exist: the Noble Council funds the army, the Novemgrod city guard has no jurisdiction, and every organization with moral authority to care about this is either too small or too connected to Dorian money to move freely.
