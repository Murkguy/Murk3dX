## 3. KEMET (THE BLACK RIVER)
**Status:** Active / Occupied Apartheid State.

Nestled amidst the lands of the Black River to the South-West, Kemet stands as the oldest civilization on Tellus. Its monuments predate the memories of the Vardan Empire.

### THE OCCUPATION
Centuries ago, Kemet receded into isolation. The Dorians seized this vulnerability and established a puppet government.

### THE TURNING POINT
With the fall of Doria to the Murk, the occupiers are trapped. Instead of retreating, they have tightened their grip, establishing a brutal Apartheid State to ensure their own survival.
* **The Rebellion:** A young Pharaoh named **Shafra-Ab** is rallying the native populace to expel the "Homeless Conquerors."

### THE KEMETAN SPIRIT
To play a Kemetan is to be proud, spiritual, and simmering with righteous anger.

**1. THE BLACK RIVER**
* **The Lifeblood:** The river is perceived as the blood of the earth itself.
* **The Magic:** Kemetan spirituality focuses on the divinity of the water. Rituals involving river mud are common.

**2. THE ANCIENT CURSE**
Scattered across Kemet are temples that predate even Kemetan memory.
* **The Danger:** Locals believe these sites are haunted by the spirits of a civilization the gods erased. To enter a Pre-Kemetan ruin is to invite a curse.

**3. HOSPITALITY AND HONOR**
* **The Custom:** It is ancient law to offer what one possesses to a traveler.
* **The Trap:** This hospitality has rules. A guest who refuses a gift commits a grave insult.

## KEY FACTIONS

* **Shafra-Ab's Liberation Front** operates through an underground network of river guides, temple priests, and grain farmers who route information, fighters, and supplies toward the Pharaoh's growing guerilla force in the southern delta. They are not yet an army. They will be.
* **The Dorian Southern Command** administers the occupation from three fortified garrison cities and is aware that the Liberation Front exists, but cannot locate its central nodes because the network is structured around the temple priesthoods, and the Dorians are unwilling to desecrate the temples — they still need the population's minimal compliance to function.
* **The Pre-Kemetan Temple Scholars** are a loose fellowship of Kemetan historians and ritual specialists who study the ancient ruins that predate even Kemetan civilization. They are the only people in the country who regularly enter the cursed sites. They know things about what's in there. They do not share this information with the Dorians.

## WHAT BRINGS YOU HERE

Kemet is the oldest country on Tellus and one of the most brutally occupied. You come here because the Pre-Kemetan ruins contain technology and knowledge that nobody else has, because the Liberation Front needs people who can move freely across borders they can't cross themselves, or because the Dorian Southern Command is paying well for intelligence work that their own people can't do without being recognized. The Black River itself is a highway — if you can navigate it without being stopped at a checkpoint.

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

1. Shafra-Ab's network needs a specific object moved from a Pre-Kemetan temple in Dorian-controlled territory to a contact in the southern delta. The object has been described as "a clay vessel sealed with resin." The Liberation Front contact who gives you this job adds, carefully: "Do not open it." The temple it's in is one the Pre-Kemetan Scholars have flagged as extremely dangerous to enter. The contact does not know this, or is pretending not to.

2. A Dorian garrison commander is paying for information that would help him identify the Liberation Front's regional coordinator. He is paying enough to be interesting. He is also collecting this information to be able to negotiate a private deal with Shafra-Ab rather than report to his superiors — the Southern Command's leadership has been ordering tactics he considers war crimes, and he wants out before the reckoning arrives.

3. A Pre-Kemetan ruin near the river delta has started doing something it wasn't doing before — at night, faint light is visible from the sealed interior. The Temple Scholars sent one person in to investigate two weeks ago. She came back different: calm in a way that was wrong, speaking in a language that isn't Kemetan or Dorian. She is currently in the care of the temple priesthood. She keeps asking for someone to go back inside.
