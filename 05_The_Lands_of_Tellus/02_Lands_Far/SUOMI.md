## 2. SUOMI (THE FROZEN NORTH)
**Status:** Active / Tribal Confederacy.

Deep in the northern reaches, where the rugged mountains embrace the eternal snow, reside the Suomi. While Novemgrod hides behind walls, the Suomi embrace the cold. They embody a spirit of freedom and strength, cultivating a warrior culture.

### THE ALTHING (The 36 Voices)
The Suomi are organized into Eighteen Tribes.
* **The Structure:** Each tribe is led by a pair: a **Joht** (Warlord) and a **Noaidi** (Shaman).
* **The Council:** Together, these thirty-six individuals form the **Althing**.
* **The Tie-Breakers:** The **Vuorenuja**—Elder Noaidi who have climbed the sacred peaks—hold the power to break deadlocks.

### THE SUOMARAN WAY
To play a Suomi is to be massive, honorable, and dangerously honest.

**1. THE SECOND SON (The Mercenary)**
Inheritance is strictly linked to the firstborn.
* **The Tradition:** Second-born children leave the tribe to seek their fortune.
* **The Reputation:** These "Suomaran" are celebrated mercenaries, often approaching 7 feet tall. A Suomaran bodyguard is a status symbol in the South.

**2. THE MOUNTAIN GODS (Tursakkaanen)**
The Suomi revere the spirits of the peaks of Aventhorn.
* **The Rite of Passage:** Aspiring leaders must climb Aventhorn. Those who survive often emerge changed, claiming to hear the whispers of the gods in the wind.

**3. THE SPRING GAMES**
Suomi society values physical might above all else.
* **The Event:** Annually, the tribes gather for grueling tests of lifting, throwing, and wrestling. To win a Spring Game is to earn a seat at the Joht’s table.

**4. HOSPITALITY AND BLOOD**
* **The Rule:** If you are a guest, you are family.
* **The Edge:** If that hospitality is abused, or if a guest lies, the peaceful demeanor vanishes. Suomi justice involves an axe.

## KEY FACTIONS

* **The Althing** is not a unified body — it is eighteen pairs of Joht and Noaidi who agree on very little except that no single tribe should dominate the others. Right now three Joht are maneuvering for alliance with Bataar's coalition to the east, and three others are maneuvering to stop them, and the remaining twelve are watching to see who wins before committing.
* **The Vuorenuja** are the elder Noaidi who hold tie-breaking authority. They are currently split on the Bataar question, which means the Althing is deadlocked. The oldest Vuorenuja, a woman named Talvikki, has not spoken publicly on the issue, which everyone finds extremely ominous.
* **The Suomaran Compact** is the informal network of second-born Suomi mercenaries operating throughout the Near Lands and Far Lands. They are not a guild — there's no formal organization, no headquarters, no dues. There's a code of conduct enforced through reputation, and a web of debts and favors stretching from Novemgrod to Kemet. They communicate mostly through the Nine Mothers' Listeners.

## WHAT BRINGS YOU HERE

Suomi is where you hire the best fighters alive and where you go when you need someone who will tell you the truth regardless of consequences. The Suomaran mercenaries are available throughout the Near Lands, but if you need to deal with the Althing directly — for political leverage, a military contract, or access to the mountain — you come north. The Suomi also sit between the Shelf and Beithar, which means anyone trying to track Bataar's movements needs a contact in the tribes.

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

1. Talvikki, the eldest Vuorenuja, has sent word that she wants to speak to "an outsider who has survived something that should have killed them." The Althing does not know she has sent this message. Her intention is unclear, but the Noaidi who delivered the message added: "She has climbed the mountain four times. The third time, she stayed for a week. We don't talk about what came back." The Althing election is in three weeks.

2. A Suomaran mercenary in Novemgrod — a woman named Hekke, registered with the Ruthenian Compact — has stopped responding to contact from her employer. Her last known job was a private security contract for a Korovladi family. The Korovladi are claiming the contract was completed and she was paid. The Suomaran Compact's informal arbitration network says she never made the deposit she always makes when a contract completes. They want to know what happened, and they want to hear it from someone who can walk into Korovladi spaces without triggering a diplomatic incident.

3. A Noaidi from the pro-Bataar faction has arrived in Novemgrod seeking to purchase a large quantity of firearms and medical supplies. She has the currency — a mix of furs and carved amber worth well over market rate. She cannot navigate the Novemgrod weapons market alone without attracting exactly the wrong kind of attention. She needs a guide. She is also, quietly, carrying a sealed message for someone in the city that she hasn't mentioned yet.
