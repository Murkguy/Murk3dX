# SAMPLE ADVENTURE: THE QUIET STREET

*A two-episode adventure for 3–4 characters at Rank 1–2.*

This adventure demonstrates the full arc of a *Murk* session: investigation in the city, a confrontation in the Northern Ruins, and a descent into the Undercroft. It is intentionally incomplete at the edges — the resolution depends on what the players do, and the most important question it raises is left unanswered. That is not a design flaw. It is the seed for the next arc.

---

## WHAT IS ACTUALLY HAPPENING

Before running Episode 1, the Director needs to know the truth.

A Divers' Guild factor named **Osta Vren** has been mapping unauthorized tunnel access points beneath the Northern Ruins — routes into the Undercroft that bypass the Guild's checkpoints and fees. Three weeks ago, he found one. He cleared it, descended twice, and on his second descent went too far. He found something in the pre-Calamity architecture below — a sealed room with a locked door propped shut from the inside. And someone, or something, inside it.

Vren told nobody except his apprentice, **Kael**, because he didn't know who to trust. He was right to be cautious.

A rogue mercenary captain named **Cord Halver**, operating out of the Northern Ruins with a small professional crew called the Quiet Street, had been watching Vren's night trips north. Halver grabbed him. Not to kill him — Vren is worth more alive as a guide. But Halver has been in the mercenary trade long enough to know that whatever Vren found below is worth considerably more than the Guild survey fees he was trying to avoid.

The players are not investigators. They are not hired to solve this. They are hired — or persuaded, or obligated — by a fourteen-year-old runner named Kael who has no one else to ask.

---

## KEY FIGURES

**Osta Vren** — Divers' Guild factor, mid-50s, meticulous and cautious. Has been filing unauthorized surveys for years without incident. Currently held by the Quiet Street in the counting house. He is scared, but he is not broken. He will cooperate with anyone who can get him out. He will not go back into the tunnel voluntarily.

**Kael** — Vren's apprentice runner, 14, carries a leather satchel with Vren's last set of contract notes and a hand-drawn map of the northern approaches. She knows two of the men who took Vren by sight — both wearing a specific mercenary badge (a copper coin with a hole punched through it). She can't go to the Shore-Men because Vren owed them money. She can't go to the Guild because Vren's surveys were unauthorized and she doesn't want him arrested. She is resourceful in the way that people who've survived Novemgrod's streets since childhood are resourceful. She notices things.

**Cord Halver** — Rogue mercenary captain. Not a villain. Practical, professional, and currently out of his depth because what Vren found is bigger than any contract he has run before. He will negotiate if given a reason to. He will not negotiate if he thinks the players are bluffing.

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**CORD HALVER — OPERATIVE**

Body d10 / Mind d8 / Spirit d6 | Tactics d8

*Prepared Ground:* While operating in a space Halver controls, all enemy checks involving movement or positioning are DR+1.

*No Loose Ends:* When Halver reduces a target to Stress 4, he immediately takes one free action — typically to reposition, disarm, or secure.

</div>

The Quiet Street has 6–8 additional members. They function as a Mob (size 6, DR 5 per target) in open combat. Halver operates as an Operative.

## EPISODE 1: THE QUIET STREET

### OPENING

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

Kael finds the players the way desperate people always find the players — at a bad time, in a bad place, looking like the only option she has left.

She's been watching them for two days. She has a list of criteria. The players meet enough of them that she's decided to take the chance.

She opens with: "I have money. Not much. But I have information, and the information is worth more than the money. I need someone to find a man named Osta Vren. He was taken four days ago. I know who has him and roughly where. I need him back before — " she stops. "Before they decide he's more useful as a warning than as a hostage."

She puts a copper coin on the table in front of them. It has a hole punched through the center.

"I took it off one of the men who grabbed him. That's their mark. They're called the Quiet Street."

</div>

### THE INVESTIGATION

The players have a name, a badge, and the knowledge that Vren was going north at night. Let them work.

**What Kael can tell them:**
- She can describe two of the men physically. Both older, professional, moved like soldiers.
- Vren had been making night trips for three weeks, heading north.
- He came back from the second-to-last trip agitated — not frightened, *excited*. He wouldn't tell her what he found.
- The abduction happened in daylight, on a street two blocks from the Guild offices. Fast and quiet.

**Information sources and what they know:**

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**DIVERS' GUILD CONTACT** — Parley DR 8 (or a favor owed)

Vren filed an unauthorized survey form three weeks ago. The Guild pulled it and sent him a warning notice — standard procedure for small operators going off the books. The form referenced a street address in the Northern Ruins, Block 14. The Guild did not follow up because Vren is small-time and the fee for the fine is more effort than it's worth.

</div>

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**SHORE-MAN (any mid-rank)** — Parley DR 10, or Barter 1 payment

The copper-coin badge belongs to the Quiet Street — a mercenary outfit that moved into the Northern Ruins about six months ago. Professional operation. They don't cause trouble on the Shelf. They take contracts that other outfits won't ask questions about. The Shore-Men leave them alone because the Shore-Men have enough problems in the parts of the city they actually patrol.

"Professional" is said the way you'd say it about a surgeon who doesn't ask where the body came from.

</div>

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**KOROVLADI GUARD** — Parley DR 13

There have been three formal complaints filed about the Quiet Street. All three were dismissed as unverifiable. The Council's position is that the Northern Ruins are outside their operational priority. What happens north of the cable district is not the Council's problem until it is.

The guard will note, if pushed, that the Council's position has been consistent for eighteen months — which is roughly when a specific Council member began receiving anonymous infrastructure donations to the southern districts.

</div>

The investigation should take one scene. The players have the address. The only question is how they approach it.

### THE NORTHERN RUINS

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

The transition happens gradually, then all at once.

The cable-walkers stop at the third cross-street north of the Foundry district. Beyond that, the iron cables above are still there — they just haven't been inspected in years, and some of them have started to sag. The fog bells are further apart. The buildings are intact but quiet in a way that is different from empty. Someone is watching from the upper floors. Two someones.

The Quiet Street has sentries. They're good at their jobs.

</div>

If the players try to approach without being seen: Stealth check, DR 10. Success means they reach the counting house perimeter unannounced. Failure means Halver knows company is coming — not who or why, just that someone is moving toward his position.

### THE COUNTING HOUSE

The Quiet Street's base is a reinforced two-story building that served as a trade counting house before the Northern Ruins became what they are. The ground floor has been staged: furniture moved to create chokepoints, windows barred, a clear firing line from the stairwell to the front door.

Halver is on the second floor. Vren is in the basement.

**The three approaches:**

**Negotiate** — Halver will talk. If the players announce themselves at a distance and make clear they want a conversation, Halver will agree to one. He is not stupid enough to turn down information. He wants to know who sent them and what they know. He will trade Vren for access to the tunnel — specifically, for a Guild survey pass that makes the descent legal. If the players can arrange that, he will shake on it and mean it. He does not kill people he doesn't need to kill.

**Deceive** — Players can claim to be Guild representatives investigating Vren's disappearance. Parley + Mind, DR 13. Success buys one scene — Halver will cooperate long enough to assess whether they're legitimate. He will see through the deception before the scene ends unless the players have compelling cover (Guild documentation, Kael's stolen contract notes, a credible story about the unauthorized survey). Failure means Halver knows immediately and the situation shifts to negotiation under tension or combat.

**Force** — The counting house is Halver's turf. *Prepared Ground* applies throughout the ground floor. The Mob of six mercenaries occupies the building; Halver holds the second floor. This is winnable but it will cost Stress. The basement door is locked (Body DR 8 to force, or find the key on a mercenary).

### WHAT VREN TELLS THEM

When found, Vren is unhurt and determined not to show how frightened he's been. He talks fast.

He found a tunnel entrance in the basement of a collapsed residential building two blocks east — Block 14, which matches the address on the Guild survey form. He cleared it. Descended twice. On the second descent, he went below the standard Undercroft level into pre-Calamity architecture. Different brick, lower ceilings, no Murk markers. *Breathable without filters.* He found a sealed door — propped shut from inside, wooden beams wedged against it. He didn't open it.

He's going to tell them: there was something on the other side of the door. Not sound, exactly. A presence. He could not explain it better than that, and he is the kind of man who does not say things he cannot explain. He spent thirty seconds at the door and then walked back to the surface as fast as he could without running.

He does not want to go back. He will tell them exactly where the tunnel entrance is. He will give them his hand-drawn map of the descent route. He will not accompany them.

*Episode 1 ends here.* The players know where the tunnel is. They know something is at the bottom of it. They have — or have not — dealt with Halver. Both outcomes lead forward.

## EPISODE 2: THE THIRD LEVEL

### OPENING

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

The building's collapse happened wrong.

Most structural failures go outward — floors sag, walls bow, the whole thing settles into rubble. This building collapsed *inward*, which means something pushed the floor up from below and then fell back. The push marks are old. Thirty years, maybe more.

The tunnel entrance is in the northeast corner of the basement. The rubble around it has been cleared recently — fresh scrape marks on the stone where someone moved debris by hand. The air coming up from below is stale but breathable. Vren's map shows three descending levels before the pre-Calamity architecture begins.

</div>

### THE DESCENT

Run the descent as three distinct zones, each with its own texture.

**Levels 1–2 (Standard Undercroft)**

Mirestone seams appear in the walls — faintly phosphorescent, blue-green, warm to the touch. The glow is not unpleasant. It is the kind of light that makes you want to look at it longer than you should.

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**SEAM-SICKNESS**

Prolonged exposure to Mirestone (more than 2 scenes in the same section) causes Seam-sickness: -1 to all checks until the character rests on the surface. This is cumulative — a character who spends 4 scenes in heavy Mirestone contact takes -2. Mention the glow. Let the players manage their own exposure. Do not announce the mechanic until someone asks or a check fails.

</div>

**The Changed (optional encounter)**

At some point between Level 1 and Level 2, the players encounter an Undercroft worker who has been down here too long. His name is Priet. His skin has gone pale in the way that happens when you stop seeing sunlight, and his eyes have adapted to the Mirestone glow in a way that makes them look wrong in direct light.

He is not hostile. He knows these tunnels better than anyone currently above ground. He will trade information for supplies — specifically, for food and any surface news. He will not go back up. He decided this two years ago and he has not regretted it.

What Priet knows:
- The pre-Calamity section below is breathable and has been for as long as he's been down here.
- The sealed door at the bottom is older than the Undercroft. He has been to it once and did not try to open it. He heard something on the other side. He is not sure what.
- There are two other sealed sections in the pre-Calamity level. He has never heard anything from those.

**Level 3 (Pre-Calamity)**

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

The carved stone gives way without warning.

One step you are in the Undercroft — rough-cut rock, Mirestone seams, the smell of old machinery — and the next you are somewhere older. The brick here is a different color, laid in a pattern that nobody currently uses. The ceiling is lower. The air is still and dry in a way that has nothing to do with filtration.

There is no Murk down here. The fog does not reach this deep. You do not need your filters. That should be a relief, and it is, and it also means you are somewhere the Calamity did not touch — which raises questions you do not have time for right now.

</div>

### THE SEALED ROOM

Vren's map leads them to a corridor ending in a heavy pre-Calamity door. The door is not locked. It is held shut by four wooden beams wedged against the inside — visible through a small gap at the top of the frame, placed by someone who knew the door opened inward, who wanted to ensure it stayed closed from within, and who did not plan on leaving.

Opening the door requires removing the beams. This takes approximately two minutes of careful work through the gap, or a Body DR 13 check to force it (the beams hold but the frame can be bent enough to clear them).

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

The room is small. A desk. A chair. Shelves that once held documents — most of the documents are still there, in stacks, in a pre-Calamity cipher that nobody currently reads without specialized tools. The shelves are labeled in the same script.

In the far corner, a cot. On the cot, a shape covered with a rough blanket that is too clean for thirty years of abandonment.

The shape is breathing.

They appear human. Late middle age, perhaps — it is difficult to judge. Their skin is wrong in a way that takes a moment to identify: it is too smooth. Not young-smooth, but preserved. There is no dust on them despite the dust on everything else in the room. They do not respond to sound. When the players bring a light source close, they respond — slowly, with evident pain, the way someone responds to a bright light after years of dark. They do not open their eyes fully. They do not speak.

Whoever this is has been in this room for thirty-two years. They are alive.

</div>

Do not explain who or what this person is. The Director should not decide before running the adventure. Let the players theorize. Their theories will be better than anything written in advance, and the best answer is always the one they arrived at themselves.

**The documents on the desk** are in pre-Calamity cipher. A Scholar check at DR 13 identifies the script as administrative — record-keeping, not personal correspondence. Full translation requires specialized tools and time. The players can take them.

### THE COMPLICATION

At some point during or after the players find the sealed room, Cord Halver arrives — or sends two of his remaining people ahead of him. This should happen at a dramatically useful moment, not as a random encounter.

Halver is not malicious. He has followed the players because he has realized that what Vren found is considerably larger than a bypass survey. He wants a piece of it. He will propose an alliance — share of whatever comes out of this, in exchange for him not telling anyone else what he's found. He is practical enough to know he can't take it by force if the players are competent, and he is pragmatic enough to prefer a deal to a fight in a confined pre-Calamity corridor.

If the players dealt fairly with Halver in Episode 1, he approaches with genuine interest in partnership. If they burned him, he arrives with leverage — he sent a message to the Guild before coming down, and retrieval is conditional on the players giving him something.

### RESOLUTION

The players decide what to do with the room, the documents, and the person on the cot. None of the options are wrong.

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**TAKING THE PERSON OUT**

They cannot walk unassisted. They react badly to Mirestone exposure in the upper levels — treat this as Seam-sickness accelerated (every scene in a Mirestone section causes 1 Stress). Getting them to the surface without killing them requires a Medic check at DR 10 and active assistance from at least one other character. What happens when they reach the surface is the beginning of the next arc, not the end of this one.

**DOCUMENTING AND LEAVING**

The players take the documents, mark the location on their maps, and wedge the door shut again. This is the cautious choice. The Schola Magisterium will want to know. So will the Guild, once word spreads. So will anyone who has been looking for this person for thirty-two years, if anyone has been looking.

**TELLING NO ONE**

A valid choice. The faction clocks tick regardless. Someone else will find this place — the tunnel is open, Halver knows about it, and Priet is still down here. The question is not whether the secret holds, but how long.

</div>

### CLOSING SCENE

Episode 2 ends when the players return to the surface. It should be quiet. Early morning. The fog bells are ringing somewhere below.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

Kael is waiting at the tunnel entrance. She sat there through the night. She has a flask of bad coffee from somewhere and she offers it to whoever comes out first without asking any questions yet.

She waits until everyone is out. Then she asks: "Is Vren safe?"

After that: "What did you find?"

What the players tell her determines what kind of Ally she becomes — or doesn't.

</div>

## DIRECTOR NOTES

**Kael** is more capable than she appears. She has survived Novemgrod's streets since she was eight. She is not helpless and she does not want to be protected — she wants to be useful. If the players treat her as an asset, she becomes one. If they dismiss her, she notices. She is resourceful in ways that will become relevant in subsequent sessions.

If the players are honest with her about what they found, she becomes a minor Ally (relationship: Grateful, +1 to Parley checks made through her contacts). If they lie or withhold without reason, she finds out — she is fourteen years old and she has been figuring out when adults are lying to her for most of her life.

**Cord Halver** is not a villain. He is a professional in a difficult business who made a reasonable call that put him in an uncomfortable position. If the players deal with him fairly across both episodes, he can become a recurring Operative-level contact with Northern Ruins expertise and connections to the rogue mercenary community. He respects competence. He does not respect cruelty.

**The person in the room** should remain unexplained through this adventure. The Director should not decide what they are before running it. Write down the players' best theories after Episode 2 and use them. The answer that feels most true is usually the one that came from the table, not from the text.

**XP Awards:**
- Episode 1 complete: 3 XP
- Episode 2 complete: 3 XP
- Bonus (protected Kael and kept her informed): +1 XP
- Bonus (resolved Halver without unnecessary violence): +1 XP
