# APATUR (THE DARK BROTHER)

**Status:** Forbidden (in some lands) / Active
**Domains:** War, Trickery, Domination
**Worshippers:** Mercenaries, Assassins, Generals, Warmongers

To the followers of Grimuus, The Great War was not a tragedy; it was the peak of human civilization. The acceleration of technology, the unity of purpose, and the purging of the weak were signs of divine progress. To them, peace is stagnation. They have worked since time immemorial to foment conflict, believing that the world only moves forward when it is bleeding.

---

## THE THIRD BROTHER HERESY
Certain forbidden scriptures claim that Grimuus is the third and youngest sibling of **Makru** and **Xelas**.

* **The Heresy:** This is anathema to the followers of the Twin Architects, but the logic is difficult to refute. Grimuus works in tandem with Makru to create wars that necessitate higher order (fortifications/weapons), and Xelas’ followers cannot deny that the greatest scientific breakthroughs happen during wartime.
* **The Trinity:** Maker, Thinker, Killer.

---

## THE TENETS OF CONFLICT
Grimuus is Lawful Evil because he follows a code, but it is a code of wolves.

* **The Engine:** "Man’s nature is that of War. Only through struggle do we move the world forward."
* **The Right:** "To master another is to grow yourself. Domination of the physical or mental is a divine right of the strong."
* **The Dark:** "A world that can be altered in the dark will not return to its light."

---

## THE WORSHIP
Grimuus is worshipped by those whose lives are defined by violence.

* **The Soldiers:** Mercenaries and Freelancers pay homage for victory.
* **The Spies:** Assassins and Spies make offerings for the success of their "Dark Alterations."
* **The State:** Even respectable Generals in Novemgrod might secretly pour a libation to Grimuus before a campaign, "just to be safe."

---

## HOLY DAYS
* **The Day of Vendril Fields (January 6th):** A celebration of the defeat of the Great Sultan by a much smaller Imperial force through tactical betrayal.
    * *The Pitfighting:* It is marked by brutal pit-fights where slaves or prisoners battle to the death.
* **The Week of Knives:** Leaders of the world mark this week with extreme caution. It is considered a holy act to murder a King or Prince during this week. If you kill a ruler on Vendril Fields, it is seen as divine proof that they were too weak to rule.

## IN THE MURK

Apatur is not a comfortable god to follow. He has no temples in Novemgrod. He has no public priests. Nobody admits to lighting a candle for him before a fight.

But the mercenaries do it anyway.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

The Suomaran sharpens her blade before the descent. She doesn't pray. She doesn't close her eyes. She just runs the stone along the edge with complete attention, like the blade is the only thing in the world, and she mutters something in old Suomi that her grandmother taught her.

She doesn't know if Apatur is real. She doesn't know if any of them are real. What she knows is that the blade is sharp, and the feeling in her hands when she sharpens it is the feeling of being ready, and whatever that feeling comes from, she is grateful for it.

She picks up the blade. She goes in.

</div>

Apatur's practical worship in the Murk and the mercenary world looks nothing like the formal cult. There are no pit fights here — just the small private rituals of people who have accepted that violence is their work and have found a theology that doesn't lie to them about that.

* **The Mercenaries' Rite:** Before entering a contract with high mortality odds, some mercenaries burn a small offering — a piece of their own gear, something they'd rather keep — as acknowledgment that Apatur may be collecting shortly. This is not surrender; it's accurate accounting.
* **The War and the Murk:** Apatur's relationship with the Black Day is the question his followers cannot answer. The Great War was, by their theology, the peak of civilization — the proof of Apatur's divine purpose. And then the Murk came, and it is still coming. Did Apatur allow the Calamity? Did he cause it? Did it simply happen, indifferent to his theology? The most honest Apatur followers say they don't know, and that the uncertainty is itself a form of devotion — faith that holds even when the god's plan is not legible.
* **The Acolyte's Dilemma:** An Acolyte who follows Apatur does not broadcast this. The Order of the Hammer considers Apatur heretical (the Third Brother Heresy). In Novemgrod, "I follow the Dark Brother" will get you investigated. The faith is practiced privately, justified quietly, and not discussed at Crown functions.
