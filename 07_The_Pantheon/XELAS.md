# XELAS (THE SILENT WATCHER)

**Status:** Active / Patron of Scholars & Esoterists
**Domains:** Knowledge, Arcana, The Future
**Relation:** The Younger Twin Brother of Makru

While Makru builds the walls of this world, his younger brother Xelas sits in his high tower, contemplating the next one. Xelas is the God of Knowing, the patron of the Magisters, the Esoterists, and the Advisors.

---

## THE GRAND DESIGN
Makru focuses on this reality, but Xelas believes this world is merely a draft.

* **The Afterlife:** Xelas questions every soul that passes. He adds their knowledge to his infinite library. If he is impressed by a soul's intellect or discovery, he keeps them in his tower to work on **The Grand Design**—the blueprint for the next reality.
* **The Danger:** This makes Xelas a detached deity. He might allow a tragedy to happen simply to see what data it produces.

---

## THE TENETS OF INQUIRY
Xelas is not evil, but he is amoral in his pursuit of truth.

* **The Collection:** "Our journey in life is to gather all knowledge we can to better shape the next world."
* **The Open Door:** "No knowledge is forbidden, even what may harm humanity here will surely aid them beyond."
* **The Whisper:** "We are guides to those in power. We must use our reason to push them into Xelas’ heart."

---

## TEMPLES OF PAPER
Even without dedicated cathedrals, Xelas has holy spaces in nearly every city.

* **The Library:** Every library, archive, and map-room is a temple to Xelas.
* **The Welcome:** His followers are openly welcomed in most cities. A Xelasian Priest is not a preacher; they are a consultant, a scientist, or a Vizier. They are the "Wise Men" standing behind the thrones of the Makru-worshipping Kings.

---

## HOLY RITES
* **Saturnus (2nd Week of January):** A week of silence. Followers work, eat, and study without speaking a single word, devoting themselves entirely to listening to the thoughts of the god.
* **The Seeking Year (Rite of Passage):** At age 16, a follower is sent out into the world. They cannot return to the temple until they bring back a piece of new information—a map to a lost ruin, a new scientific formula, or a secret stolen from a rival.

---

## XELAS AND THE SCHOLA MAGISTERIUM

The Schola Magisterium is not a religious institution. High Magister Valeska would be irritated if you called it one. And yet.

Every senior Schola Magister has completed what the institution calls a "Seeking Year." Every Schola archive follows the Xelasian principle that no knowledge is forbidden. Every Schola researcher operates under the tenet that their purpose is to "gather all knowledge we can." The Schola's founding charter, written five centuries ago by a man whose name was later removed from the document, includes language directly parallel to Xelas' Tenets of Inquiry.

Nobody talks about this officially.

* **The Uneasy Coexistence:** Xelas followers and Schola Magisters study the same things — magic, esoterica, history, natural philosophy — for different reasons. The follower seeks knowledge to prepare the next world. The Magister seeks knowledge to understand and control this one. In practice, they collaborate constantly and argue about methodology even more constantly. The Schola's library is, by Xelasian doctrine, a temple. Valeska has never acknowledged this framing. She also has never refused to let the Xelasian priest at the Cathedral Quarter's observatory use the Schola's star charts.
* **The Resonance Question:** Xelas followers believe that everything that happens is data for the Grand Design. The Resonance — whatever is buried under the Palatine Regium — is the single most significant unexplained phenomenon on the Shelf. Xelas priests consider its study the highest possible calling. The Schola considers it classified. This is the primary point of friction between the two institutions: the Schola has information about the Resonance that it will not share, and the Xelasians believe that withholding knowledge is the only true heresy.
* **Valeska's Religion:** High Magister Valeska does not discuss her personal theology. She was observed once, by a junior Magister who knew better than to ask about it, placing a single unlit candle in a glass holder of the exact design used in Xelasian personal shrines. The junior Magister has not mentioned this to anyone. He also hasn't stopped thinking about it.
