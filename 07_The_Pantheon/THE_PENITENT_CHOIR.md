# THE PENITENT CHOIR (THE ORPHANS OF LE VIDES)

**Status:** Active / Schismatic Faith
**Domains:** Peace, Grave, Atonement
**Origin:** Pytharis (The Shattered Church of Le Vides)

The Penitent Choir is a religion born from abandonment. Originally the priesthood of Le Vides in Pytharis, they were left broken when their god went silent during the Calamity.

While the rest of Pytharis tries to ignore the silence, the Choir has interpreted it as a divine judgment. They believe Le Vides did not die; he turned his back on humanity because of the cruelty of the War.

---

## THE TENETS OF GUILT
The Choir believes that the only way to lure their god back is to prove humanity is worth saving.

* **The Burden:** "Man has ruined the world. We must atone through acts of love and suffering."
* **The Calling:** "Every Faith is above the rabble, for the followers are called to their destiny."
* **The Warning:** "Man cannot become Gods. Such hubris dooms the world."

---

## THE WORKS OF MERCY
Despite their gloomy theology, the Penitent are goodly folk. They run the poor houses in the slums of Novemgrod and Kemet. They are the steadfast guardians of the vulnerable, washing the feet of lepers and feeding the hungry when the Magisters turn away.

---

## THE SCHISM (FLAGELLANTS)
The reformation has not gone without controversy.

* **The Main Body:** Focuses on acts of charity and singing dirges to the silent sky.
* **The Violent Sects:** The most fervent believe that charity is not enough—only blood can pay the debt. These sects have adopted flagellation ceremonies, whipping themselves in public squares. While considered anathema to the greater faith, their numbers are growing.

### THE TWO CHOIRS (IN NOVEMGROD)

The Penitent Choir's Novemgrod presence has split along lines that the central leadership in Pytharis is unwilling to formally acknowledge.

**The Charitable Mainstream** operates out of the Central Hall in the Rungs. They run the largest free kitchen in the Boiler district, a medical clinic that operates without asking for papers, and a network of safe housing for people who can't afford Diver staging fees but have nowhere else to sleep. Their congregation is the largest single religious gathering in the city — larger than the Makru High Temple's Sunday attendance, which the Orthodox priesthood is very quietly furious about. Their leader, a former Kemet exile named Brother Cassin, preaches atonement through service and is deeply suspicious of the Flagellant movement.

**The Flagellant Wing** in Novemgrod does not have an official address. Their public ceremonies — self-inflicted punishment performed in open plazas — began two years ago with small gatherings and have grown to crowds of several hundred. They are not preaching violence toward others. They are preaching suffering as currency: enough accumulated pain, they believe, will force Le Vides to turn around. The mainstream Choir is horrified by this on principle and terrified by it on a practical level, because the Flagellants are drawing members away from the charity work.

The schism has a breaking point approaching: the Flagellant wing's leader, a woman who goes by Sister Marek, has begun claiming to receive direct communication from Le Vides — something the mainstream theology says is impossible, given that Le Vides turned away. If she is lying, she is a dangerous fraud with an increasingly armed following. If she is telling the truth, the entire post-Calamity theology of the Choir collapses.

Brother Cassin is losing sleep over both possibilities.

## HOLY DAYS
* **Varied:** Because the church shattered, there is no unified calendar. Each sect celebrates (or mourns) on different days, usually marking specific tragedies of the War.
