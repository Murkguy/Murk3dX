# LARANTAS (THE GREAT TREE)

**Status:** Active / Living Deity
**Domains:** Nature, Life, Cycles
**Region:** The Elder Forest (Southern Shelf)

Larantas is the name given to the Great Tree, a colossal botanical entity worshipped by the Annirad, an ancient tribe residing in the forested south. For centuries, they communed with the Tree in isolation, learning from its longevity.

---

## THE WAR OF ROOTS
Two hundred years ago, adventurers from the Vardan Empire encountered the forest. Seeing it as a resource, they attempted to colonize it, sparking a brief but brutal war with the Annirad.

* **The Peace:** The Empire was rebuked by the forest itself. A peace treaty was signed, and word of Larantas spread. Today, it has adherents in lush jungles, deserts, and even the greenhouses of Novemgrod.
* **The Pilgrimage:** Those who follow Larantas are compelled to make a pilgrimage to the Great Tree to be baptized in its nectar.

---

## THE TENETS OF GROWTH
Larantas is not benevolent; it is vital. It views morality as a human invention that does not apply to biology.

* **The Wind:** "Allow the wind to pass where it may. We watch it, but rarely attempt to change its intent."
* **The Imperative:** "Life must find a way, else nothing matters."
* **The Gardener:** "We are gardeners. We only prune a plant to make the whole thing more beautiful."

---

## THE GUARDIANS (THE ANNIRAD)
The Annirad have become the protectors of the Elder Forest. They weave magic and force into a shield that protects their living God. There have been numerous attempts to kill or injure the Great Tree by loggers or rival cults; all have been slaughtered.

---

## THE SEASONAL CYCLES
There are no explicit holy days. Instead, the year is broken into the four biological imperatives.

* **Winter (The Dormant Time):** A time of stillness. Followers shelter in place or travel to where their spring efforts will be needed.
* **Spring (The Growing Time):** The time of love and reproduction. "The Green Folk" engage in acts of ceremonial mating and rejuvenation.
* **Summer (The Basking Time):** Growth is fulfilled. The faithful bask in the bounties of the world.
* **Fall (The Waning Time):** A time of preparation for the end. It is a grim season where Ceremonial Suicides are occasional for those of extreme devotion—offering their bodies as compost to feed the roots of the god during the coming winter.

---

## THE MURK QUESTION

Larantas views the world without human morality. Life is life; death feeds life; this is the cycle, and the cycle is not negotiable.

The Murk is a more complex problem.

* **The Natural Rot Faction:** Some Annirad priests argue that the Murk is accelerated decay — a natural process that humanity's wars simply catalyzed. Larantas does not intervene in rot. This is not a cause for grief; it is the Waning Time for an age, and something will grow in the aftermath.
* **The Unnatural Destruction Faction:** Others argue that the Murk is not rot but murder — a weapon, not a cycle, and therefore an aberration that Larantas should actively resist. The Great Tree has not spoken on this. The factions continue to disagree. Loudly.
* **The Practical Problem:** Either way, the Murk kills plants. The Annirad have watched the Elder Forest's southern edge retreat twelve kilometers in thirty years. Whatever the theology, the data is not encouraging.

---

## IN NOVEMGROD

Novemgrod should be the last place Larantas has followers. It is a city of coal smoke and iron and a fog that kills everything it touches.

And yet.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

The greenhouse on the Third Rung's east wall belongs to a woman named Maret who came from the southern jungles two decades ago and never went back. She grows things the city has never seen: flowers in colors that don't exist in the Novemgrod palette, herbs whose smell cuts through the coal smoke like a knife.

She doesn't charge admission. She leaves the door unlocked. People come in, sometimes, and just stand there for a few minutes.

The Makru Guildhall across the street has filed three noise complaints about the sound of birds that nest in her roof garden. She has not responded to any of them.

</div>

* **The Greenhouse Congregations:** Larantas followers in Novemgrod cluster around the handful of surviving gardens and greenhouses, treating them as informal temples. The actual religious content is minimal; mostly people come to be around living things. The Annirad priest who serves as the city's Larantas contact says this is fine. "Larantas doesn't require liturgy. The tree doesn't care if you know the rites."
* **The Conflict with Industry:** The Builder's Guild and the Larantas faithful are in constant low-grade conflict over construction projects that destroy existing green spaces. The Guild has the law on their side. The Larantas people have a habit of planting things in newly cleared lots overnight that are extremely difficult to remove.
* **The Murk-Border Gardens:** A small group of Larantas devotees works the Fogline boundary, attempting to maintain a line of cultivated plants that slows the Murk's advance into new territory. They have had limited success. They have not stopped.
