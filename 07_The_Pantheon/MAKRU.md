# MAKRU (THE GOLDEN LAW)

**Status:** Active / Patron of Rulers & Builders
**Domains:** Forge, Order, Authority
**Major Centers of Worship:** Turcia (State Religion), Novemgrod (Guilds), Doria (Noble Houses)

Makru is the Eldest of the Twin Brothers, the eminent builder who constructed the eternal Towers in which his younger sibling, Xelas, rests. He is the divine patron of civilization itself — the force that separates the City from the Wild.

To follow Makru is not a spiritual comfort. It is a contract. You build, you obey, you endure. In exchange, the world holds together. The moment you stop, the Murk creeps in.

---

## THE DIVINE RIGHT

Makru is the god of Kings. Many Princes and Lords across the Shelf trace their right to rule back to a singular historical event called **The Dictate of Makru**.

* **The Legend:** In the ancient world, Makru granted the "Right to Rule" to 12 Adventurers. The descendants of these twelve form the Great Houses that still dominate the politics of Doria, Ruthenia, and Pytharis today. To disobey a Lord is to disobey Makru.
* **The Implication:** This doctrine is enormously convenient for those already in power. Scholars who question it tend to have short careers — or shorter lives.

---

## THE TENETS OF IRON

Makru is not a merciful deity; he is a just one. He demands diligence, and he despises entropy.

* **The Forge of Will:** "We are the craftsmen of our own reality. It is the force of will that leads man to his destiny."
* **The Static Order:** "We cannot allow the defiance of fate or the natural order."
* **The Tool:** "As the craftsman, we must always seek out new tools in which to forge our will."

---

## TEMPLES OF INDUSTRY

Makru has many ornate temples, but they look more like fortresses. His followers use stone as a display of power. Every castle, bunker, and city wall in the Near Lands likely bears the Icon of Makru — the Hammer or the Gear — upon its keystone.

These are not places of quiet contemplation. They are civic institutions: courts of law, guild halls, workshops where apprentices labor under the eye of a High Artificer. The line between a prayer and a work order is deliberately blurred. Devotion is demonstrated with calloused hands.

### THE ETERNAL ENEMY

Makru is the great enemy of **The Charred One**. One seeks to build; the other seeks to burn.

* **The Conflict:** Makru's followers (The Order of the Hammer) actively hunt Charred Cultists. It is a war of construction versus arson. In Novemgrod, if a factory burns down, the Makru Priesthood will hunt the culprit with terrifying efficiency.
* **The Unspoken Question:** Every Makru priest knows the Black Day happened. The Murk swallowed half the old world. Some quietly wonder whether their god failed to prevent it — and none dare ask the question aloud.

---

## HOLY DAYS

* **Hammermass (April 14th):** A holy week of creation. It concludes with a celebratory display of the week's labor — parades of new machines, unveiling of statues, and demonstrations of architectural prowess. In Novemgrod, the Builder's Guild holds open competitions. The prize for best craftwork is a Makru iron seal: the closest thing to a license to print money in the Near Lands.
* **Recitation Day (November 5th):** A somber day, enforced by law in Turcia and by custom in noble houses elsewhere. All citizens must publicly recite their oaths of loyalty to their Lords and Masters. Failure to attend is technically heresy. Nobody has been executed for it in fifty years — but the Church keeps records.

## IN NOVEMGROD

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

The Makru Cleric at the Builder's Guild intake window doesn't look holy. He looks like an administrator. Grey coat, ink-stained fingers, the faint smell of sawdust and old paper. He stamps your work permit without looking up. "Bring this to the Third Rung Hall. You work under Foreman Brecht. You miss three days without leave, the seal is revoked."

He still hasn't looked at you.

You leave, permit in hand, and wonder who exactly you just made a deal with.

</div>

Makru's practical power in Novemgrod runs through two institutions: **the Builder's Guild** and **the Makru Orthodox Church**.

* **The Builder's Guild** holds licensing authority over virtually every construction, repair, and heavy manufacturing operation in the city. They tithe to the Church; the Church legitimizes their monopoly. Neither talks about the circular logic.
* **The Orthodox Church** runs the civil courts for the middle Rungs. Debt disputes, contract enforcement, inheritance claims — all adjudicated by Makru priests, under Makru law, in Makru buildings. Prince Alexei technically has judicial authority over all of this. In practice, he does not have the clerks.
* **Tension with the Penitent Choir:** Makru's Orthodox priesthood and the Penitent Choir agree on very little. The Choir is itinerant, populist, and suspicious of wealth. The Orthodox see them as radicals who undermine the social order. Both operate in Novemgrod. Both want the Crown's endorsement. Neither has it, because Prince Alexei is too smart to choose.
* **The Acolyte's Place:** A Makru Acolyte in Novemgrod is a clerk, a magistrate, a building inspector, or a guild enforcer depending on their posting. They carry actual institutional authority. People listen — not from spiritual awe, but because a Makru seal can open or close a city gate.
