# FAITH: THE COVENANT

**"The silence of the Gods is not an absence. It is a test."**

Faith in *The Murk* is not a weapon; it is a shield. While Esoterists tear the world apart with alien calculations, Acolytes hold it together with sheer Will. Your power does not manifest as fireballs. It manifests as **Providence** — the bullet that misses, the wound that closes, the shadow that retreats.

---

## THE THEOLOGY OF THE MUNDANE

This is the most important truth about miracles in The Murk:

**They look like accidents.**

A Patron does not rewrite physics. A Patron nudges probability — the same probability that runs the universe — just enough to produce the desired outcome. The guard trips on a loose cobblestone. The disease breaks on its own over forty-eight hours, which sometimes happens. The support beam fails at precisely the right moment, which old wood does occasionally. The enemy's gun jams, which guns do.

This is not a limitation. It is a design.

The gods of Tellus operate through **plausible deniability** — partly because the divine world has its own rules about direct intervention (the same laws that require Esoterists to steal their power, gods cannot simply rewrite outcomes without consequence), and partly because the Acolyte's role is to be a *witness*, not a spectacle. The miracle happens. The patient recovers. The Acolyte knows why. The patient calls it luck, constitution, or good medicine. The guard who tripped calls it clumsiness. The world keeps its comfortable explanations.

The Acolyte does not get credit. The Acolyte gets results.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

*Sister Aldric of the Penitent Choir was never caught performing a miracle in twenty years of field work. She healed eleven cases of Lung Rot, prevented two executions, and pulled four people back from the edge of fatal Stress. None of those people attributed it to her. She considered this the highest possible endorsement of her work.*

*Her order keeps records. The records are sealed. The point of the records is not to prove the miracles happened — the point is to remind the next generation of Acolytes that they will spend their careers being the reason things worked out, and never receiving the explanation.*

</div>

This design principle also creates a deliberate separation between what **the faithful know** and what **the world sees**. Inside the church, inside the covenant, Acolytes share detailed accounts of interventions — the specifics of what was asked, what threshold was met, how the miracle resolved. Outside that circle, the same events are described in secular language. This is not deception. It is discretion. The gods are not performing for the crowd.

## 1. THE DEVOTION DIE

Your connection to the Divine is measured by your **DEVOTION DIE**.

* **Starting Value:** **d8**.
* **Progression:** You can step this up (d10, d12) by spending XP (12 XP per step).
* **Function:** Determines your maximum Favor cap, the size of your Petition dice, and the weight your prayers carry in the divine order.

A higher Devotion Die does not mean you cast bigger fireballs. It means the probability curves bend further in your favor when you ask. A Rank 1 Acolyte with a d8 asks for the guard to look away. An Archdeacon with a d12 asks for the storm to change direction.

---

## 2. THE HOLY BAROMETER (FAVOR)

Favor is a living resource. It fluctuates based on your conduct — not your skill, not your power, your *conduct*.

### A. THE MORNING PRAYER (STARTING FAVOR)
At the start of every Session, roll your **DEVOTION DIE**.
* **The Result:** This is your **Starting Favor** for the session.
* **Interpretation:** A low roll implies spiritual distance — the silence between prayers. A high roll implies you are filled with purpose, aligned with your Patron's will in a way that feels physical.
* **The Cap:** Your Favor can never exceed the maximum value of your Devotion Die (d8 cap = 8, d10 cap = 10, d12 cap = 12).

### B. THE TENETS (GAINING FAVOR)
Favor regenerates when you act in deliberate accordance with your Patron's nature.
* **Trigger:** Fulfilling a Tenet of your God in a significant, risky, or dramatic way.
* **Effect:** Gain **+1 Favor**.
* The action must be intentional and carry some cost or risk. Going out of your way to fulfill a Tenet in a scene where it was easy to ignore earns nothing. Doing it when it costs you something earns the Favor.

### C. THE TRANSGRESSIONS (LOSING FAVOR)
* **Trigger:** Violating the nature of your Patron — cowardice, greed, cruelty, depending on the God.
* **Effect:** Lose **1 Favor** immediately.
* The Director calls Transgressions, not the player. A player who argues that their action wasn't really a Transgression has already answered the question.

### D. THE HOLLOW STATE
When your Favor drops to **0**, you are **Hollow**.
* **The Risk:** If you commit a Transgression while at 0 Favor, the Covenant breaks.
* **The Penalty:** Take **d6 Stress (Guilt)** immediately.
* **The Condition:** You become **Forsaken**. You cannot gain Favor or use Miracles until you perform a significant Atonement — a narrative quest, a sacrifice, or a public act of devotion proportional to the Transgression.

## 3. THE PETITION (THE MECHANIC)

Miracles are not guaranteed. You must ask, and the Gods must choose to answer.

**THE LAW OF AMBIGUITY:**
A Petition must always be explainable as luck, fate, or coincidence. If you ask for physics to break — flight, fire from nothing, resurrection of someone dead more than a few minutes — the Petition fails automatically. The gods work *through* the world, not around it.

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**HOW TO PETITION**

1. **State your Intent.** Keep it in mundane terms: "I want the guard to look away." "I want this door to open." "I want him to survive."
2. **The Director sets the Threshold.** How improbable is this outcome?
3. **Spend Favor.** Declare how much you are spending (minimum 1).
4. **Roll that many Devotion Dice.** Sum the total.

| Difficulty | Threshold | Example |
|:--|:--|:--|
| **The Nudge** | 5+ | A rusty lock breaks; a guard gets distracted; you find one round of ammo left. |
| **The Stroke of Luck** | 10+ | A beam falls on an enemy; a disease remits; a beast ignores you entirely. |
| **The Intervention** | 15+ | You survive a lethal fall; an entire squad panics; a storm covers your escape. |

- **Success:** The Miracle occurs. It looks like coincidence. You narrate the mundane explanation.
- **Failure (The Silence):** The Favor is spent, nothing happens. The God heard. The God chose not to answer — this time.

</div>

### READING THE SILENCE

When a Petition fails, the Director should not simply say "nothing happens." The Silence is meaningful. It means the Patron heard the request and declined it — because the timing was wrong, because the cost was too high even for the divine, or because what you asked for would have led to a worse outcome than the one you feared.

An Acolyte who understands their Patron learns to read the Silence. A Makru Acolyte whose petition to protect a door fails might understand that the door is supposed to fall — that the Order requires what comes through it. A Penitent Choir Acolyte whose petition to save a life fails might understand that the death serves the cycle in ways they cannot see.

This does not make the Silence comfortable. It makes it meaningful.

## 4. THE PATRONS

*Choose one. This defines your moral compass in the Murk.*

### A. MAKRU (THE IRON JUDGE)
*The God of Walls, Law, and Stasis. Makru believes that the Murk is Chaos, and the only defense is rigid, unyielding Order.*

* **TENETS (Gain +1 Favor):**
    * **The Pillar:** Physically holding a defensive position or blocking a path to protect others.
    * **The Gavel:** Enforcing a harsh law or truth, even when it is emotionally difficult.
    * **The Builder:** Spending time to repair a structure, barricade, or weapon.
* **TRANSGRESSIONS (Lose 1 Favor):**
    * **The Crack:** Allowing a defense to fall due to negligence.
    * **The Exception:** Showing mercy to a "Chaotic" entity when Law demands judgment.
    * **The Riot:** Participating in disorder or panic.
* **ATONEMENT:** *The Tithe of Sweat.* Spend a scene doing hard, unthanked labor without rest.

### B. THE PENITENT CHOIR (THE MOURNERS)
*The Collective Spirits of the First Martyrs. To be holy is not to avoid pain, but to share it so that no one suffers alone.*

* **TENETS (Gain +1 Favor):**
    * **The Intercession:** Taking physical damage meant for an ally.
    * **The Last Rite:** Stopping in danger to offer comfort to the dying.
    * **The Charity:** Giving away vital resources, leaving yourself with nothing.
* **TRANSGRESSIONS (Lose 1 Favor):**
    * **The Coward:** Fleeing danger while others are at risk.
    * **The Hoard:** Keeping resources when others are in need.
    * **The Silence:** Ignoring a plea for help.
* **ATONEMENT:** *The Confession.* Reveal your deepest secret or shame to make yourself vulnerable.

### C. THE NINE MOTHERS (THE TURSAKKAANEN)
*The Goddesses of Fate, Nature, and the Cycle. They despise the industrial and the undead.*

* **TENETS (Gain +1 Favor):**
    * **The Purge:** Destroying a source of "Unnatural" corruption (Undead, Murk-tech).
    * **The Old Way:** Solving a lethal problem using only natural tools and wits.
    * **The Cycle:** Protecting a birth or granting a "Good Death."
* **TRANSGRESSIONS (Lose 1 Favor):**
    * **The Abomination:** Using Necromancy or Murk-tech.
    * **The Waste:** Killing without purpose or leaving a kill to rot.
    * **The Hubris:** Attempting to dominate nature rather than coexist.
* **ATONEMENT:** *The Cold Walk.* Spend a night exposed to the elements without shelter or fire.

### D. THE CHARRED ONE (THE MAD GOD)
*Aspects: Entropy, Fire, Nihilism. Its followers are not builders; they are accelerants.*

* **TENETS (Gain +1 Favor):**
    * **The Arson:** Setting fire to a significant structure.
    * **The Unmaking:** Destroying a unique Relic or historical record.
    * **The Embrace:** Walking into a hazard without protection to prove faith.
* **TRANSGRESSIONS (Lose 1 Favor):**
    * **Preservation:** Saving a legacy from destruction.
    * **Mercy:** Preventing inevitable death.
    * **Hesitation:** Failing to destroy when the opportunity arises.
* **ATONEMENT:** *The Pyre of Self.* Subject yourself to a ritual burning (2 Ranks of Suffering, Physical).

### E. XELAS (THE YOUNGER BROTHER)
*Aspects: Knowledge, Creation, Light. Xelas believes the Murk is a puzzle to be solved.*

* **TENETS (Gain +1 Favor):**
    * **The Discovery:** Uncovering lost technology or magic.
    * **The Illumination:** Revealing a hidden truth, regardless of fallout.
    * **The Lesson:** Teaching an NPC or Player a new skill.
* **TRANSGRESSIONS (Lose 1 Favor):**
    * **Suppression:** Destroying books or concealing knowledge.
    * **Ignorance:** Acting on superstition rather than logic.
    * **Stagnation:** Refusing to investigate a mystery.
* **ATONEMENT:** *The Dark Fast.* Spend a Downtime Scene blindfolded and silent.

### F. LARANTAS (THE GREAT TREE)
*Aspects: The Green, The Rot, Symbiosis. Civilization is a sickness; roots will reclaim all stone.*

* **TENETS (Gain +1 Favor):**
    * **The Reclamation:** Destroying machinery or stone to let nature in.
    * **The Cycle:** Turning a corpse into fertilizer.
    * **The Symbiosis:** Protecting a beast or plant at cost to yourself.
* **TRANSGRESSIONS (Lose 1 Favor):**
    * **Sterility:** Creating a sterile environment.
    * **Waste:** Killing without using the corpse.
    * **Separation:** Walling yourself off from nature.
* **ATONEMENT:** *The Rooting.* Bury yourself in earth for 24 hours.

### G. APATUR (THE WAR GOD)
*Aspects: Struggle, Dominion, Iron. Only in the crucible of war can the soul be forged.*

* **TENETS (Gain +1 Favor):**
    * **The Challenge:** Defeating a powerful enemy in single combat.
    * **The Conquest:** Seizing territory or forcing surrender.
    * **The Forge:** Surviving a blow that should have killed you.
* **TRANSGRESSIONS (Lose 1 Favor):**
    * **Cowardice:** Surrendering or fleeing.
    * **Peace:** Choosing diplomacy when martial options existed.
    * **Betrayal:** Stabbing an ally in the back.
* **ATONEMENT:** *The Gauntlet.* Start a fight with superior numbers (2:1) and survive alone.

### H. THE MIDNIGHT LADY (THE VEILED MATRON)
*Aspects: Secrets, Shadows, The Void. She protects the hidden.*

* **TENETS (Gain +1 Favor):**
    * **The Secret:** Uncovering a secret someone tried to kill to keep.
    * **The Veil:** Completing a mission without being seen.
    * **The Whisper:** Using blackmail or secrets to defeat an enemy.
* **TRANSGRESSIONS (Lose 1 Favor):**
    * **Exposure:** Being caught sneaking or stealing.
    * **The Blurt:** Revealing a secret to the unworthy.
    * **The Spotlight:** Seeking fame or credit.
* **ATONEMENT:** *The Blind Vigil.* Wear a blindfold for an entire Episode.

# MIRACLES

Miracles are not spells. They are not formulas of physics. They are Interventions — the gods rearranging the existing pieces of the world to produce a different outcome.

* **No Roll Required:** Unless specified, Miracles just happen — no attack roll, no contested check.
* **The Cost:** Favor must be spent before the effect.
* **The Range:** Touch or Voice (Hearing Range).
* **The Appearance:** Every Miracle has a mundane explanation. The Director and player collaborate to name it.

## 1. THE UNIVERSAL LITURGY
*Rites known to all Acolytes, regardless of Patron.*

### MINOR MIRACLES (COST: 1 FAVOR)
* **RITE OF SOLACE:** Remove **1d6 Stress** from a target's Mind or Spirit track. Cannot heal Physical Stress. *(Looks like: the panic subsides. The shaking stops. They take a breath and remember they can do this.)*
* **RITE OF THE LANTERN:** Illuminate a Near Zone for 1 Hour with **True Sunlight** that burns undead tissue and halts supernatural regeneration. *(Looks like: you hold up your lantern and for some reason the fog doesn't creep back in.)*
* **RITE OF PASSAGE (LAST RITES):** Sanctify a corpse. It cannot be raised as Undead or possessed by an outside force. *(Looks like: a prayer. Just a prayer.)*
* **RITE OF CLARITY:** Remove one Mental Condition (Fear, Confusion, Charm, or Sleep). *(Looks like: they just snapped out of it. People do sometimes.)*

### MAJOR MIRACLES (COST: 2 FAVOR)
* **RITE OF SANCTUARY:** Create a perimeter that lesser Murk-beasts will not cross for 1 Night. *(Looks like: fortunate geography. The creatures went around.)*
* **RITE OF PURIFICATION:** Target makes a Body Save with **+1d6** to cure Poison, Disease, Blindness, or Paralysis. *(Looks like: the fever broke faster than expected. The venom didn't take hold the way it usually does.)*
* **RITE OF THE VIGIL:** Target cannot be **Surprised** and acts first in Initiative for the Scene. *(Looks like: they were already alert. They had a feeling something was coming.)*

### GRAND MIRACLES (COST: 3 FAVOR)
* **RITE OF EXORCISM:** Target a Spirit or Daemon. Roll Devotion Die vs. Spirit Die (or Severity DR).
    * *Success:* Entity is Banished or Stunned.
    * *Failure:* Entity takes **2d6 True Damage**. *(Looks like: nothing. The entity simply felt something push against it — the accumulated weight of someone's absolute refusal to let it stay.)*
* **RITE OF PROVIDENCE (Reaction):** Used when an ally Botches or fails a Save resulting in death. **The roll is ignored.** The action never happened. *(Looks like: the gun misfired at the critical moment. The ground shifted and the fall was shorter than it should have been. The blade caught on a belt buckle nobody knew was there.)*

## 2. THE PATRON RITES
*Miracles specific to your chosen God. These stack with the Universal Liturgy.*

### MAKRU (The Iron Judge)
* **THE JUDGE'S EYE (1F):** Instantly know if target is lying, armed, or guilty of a specific crime. *(Looks like: you asked the right questions.)*
* **THE LINE (2F):** A threshold becomes physically impassable for lesser enemies and Murk-entities for one Scene. *(Looks like: they simply won't come through. They find reasons to go around.)*
* **THE EDICT (3F):** Issue a one-word command to an enemy leader ("STOP." "KNEEL." "DROP."). They must obey on a failed Spirit Save. *(Looks like: your voice carried authority they weren't expecting. It happens.)*

### THE PENITENT CHOIR (The Mourners)
* **BEAR THE WITNESS (1F):** *Reaction.* Take damage meant for an ally onto yourself, unreduced. *(Looks like: you stepped in front of it.)*
* **CLEAR THE CLOUD (2F):** Instantly cure Fear, Panic, or Madness in a target. *(Looks like: a moment of stillness. The words were exactly right.)*
* **REFUSAL OF THE GRAVE (3F):** *Reaction.* An ally who would die is left at 1 Health, stable. *(Looks like: they weren't as far gone as everyone thought. The artery missed. The dosage was just short of lethal.)*

### THE NINE MOTHERS (The Tursakkaanen)
* **WILD SANCTUARY (1F):** Natural beasts ignore you and will not attack unless provoked. *(Looks like: you know how to move. Animals sense confidence.)*
* **THE KNOT (2F):** Force an enemy to re-roll a successful die. *(Looks like: they slipped. A gust of wind. The light shifted.)*
* **THE STORM (3F):** Summon weather. Enemies are Blinded and slowed; allies are unaffected. *(Looks like: the weather on the Shelf has always been unpredictable.)*

### THE CHARRED ONE (The Mad God)
* **THE CRACK (1F):** A non-magical object shatters the next time it is used. *(Looks like: it was already on the edge. Happens to old gear all the time.)*
* **AURA OF DREAD (2F):** Lesser enemies refuse to approach or attack you for the Scene. *(Looks like: something in how you stand. The way you look at them.)*
* **DOOMED FATE (3F):** The next damage the target takes is **Maximized** — every die rolls its maximum value. *(Looks like: very bad luck.)*

### XELAS (The Younger Brother)
* **ILLUMINATION (1F):** Light reveals all Hidden Things in Near Range — doors, concealed enemies, traps. *(Looks like: you knew where to look.)*
* **OPEN THE WAY (2F):** Instantly open any door or mechanism, regardless of lock or seal. *(Looks like: the lock was older than it looked. The mechanism had a flaw.)*
* **THE SUDDEN INSIGHT (3F):** Ask the Director for the solution to the current problem. The answer must be achievable with what the party currently has. *(Looks like: the pieces clicked together. It does that sometimes.)*

### LARANTAS (The Great Tree)
* **ROOTED (1F):** You cannot be Knocked Down or Moved physically for the Scene. *(Looks like: you found your footing.)*
* **OVERGROWTH (2F):** Summon vines, roots, or debris to create Heavy Cover or block paths. *(Looks like: the building was already compromised. Old construction.)*
* **SYMBIOSIS (3F):** Allies become immune to Poison, Gas, and Exhaustion for the Scene. *(Looks like: you found the right ventilation. The compound was diluted.)*

### APATUR (The War God)
* **RALLYING CRY (1F):** Allies gain **+1d6** to their next Action. *(Looks like: morale. Sometimes the words are exactly right.)*
* **IRON SKIN (2F):** Gain **Hardness 4** — reduce all incoming Physical Damage by 4, minimum 0, for the Scene. *(Looks like: adrenaline. Tunnel vision. The body can do remarkable things under pressure.)*
* **KNEEL (3F):** Enemies in Near Range must make a Spirit Save or Surrender — drop weapons, go Prone. *(Looks like: the tide of battle turned. They lost the will at the wrong moment.)*

### THE MIDNIGHT LADY (The Veiled Matron)
* **UNNOTICED (1F):** Enemies will not target you if you don't attack or speak. *(Looks like: you're just not interesting enough. People overlook what they don't understand.)*
* **THE VEIL (2F):** The entire group becomes effectively invisible until an attack is made. *(Looks like: the fog was thicker there. Nobody was looking.)*
* **THE VOID (3F):** All enemies in the area are Blinded and Deafened for one Scene. Allies see in dim twilight. *(Looks like: a power failure. Happens in the deep Murk. Nobody was surprised.)*
