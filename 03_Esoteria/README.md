# BOOK III: ESOTERIA — THE FORBIDDEN SCIENCE

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

*"Magic is not a miracle. A miracle is a gift. What you are doing is a theft — you are stealing energy from the structure of reality itself. The universe will notice. The universe will collect."*

— Archmagus Petyr Vann, address to the Schola Magisterium, Year of the Fog

</div>

---

Every culture in Tellus has a word for it. The Ruthenic frontier soldiers call it *vraz* — the force that kills from the inside. The Kemet scholar-priests call it *sekhem*, the forbidden current. The people of Novemgrod just call it *the wrong kind of physics*.

The Schola Magisterium calls it **Esoteria**, and they have spent four centuries trying to make it respectable.

They have not succeeded.

Esoteria is not a gift from the gods. It is not inherited bloodline magic. It is a **science** — specifically, the application of Vardan geometric formulae to the physical laws of the universe. The Vardan Empire discovered, sometime in the fifth century of their rule, that the laws of physics were not laws at all. They were *tendencies*. Habits. Defaults that the universe fell back on when nothing was forcing it to behave differently.

The formulae force it to behave differently.

The price is that the formulae run through the caster's nervous system. The universe's resistance — its refusal to be rewritten — manifests as raw, catastrophic pain. The Schola calls this feedback **Agony**. They use a clinical term because clinical terms make it easier to ask students to endure it.

This chapter covers everything a Director and player need to understand how magic works in The Murk — what it costs, what it can do, and what happens when it goes wrong.

---

# CHAPTER 1: THE COST OF POWER (AGONY)

The most important rule about casting in this system is also the most counterintuitive:

**The spell always works.**

There is no failed cast. There is no fizzle. When an Esoterist declares a Principle and pays the cost, the formula executes — the wall of stone moves, the man's bones begin to decay, the arc of lightning finds its target. The universe has no say in whether the effect occurs. The only question is whether the caster survives the act of forcing it.

This is a deliberate design choice. Casting a spell in The Murk is never a gamble on *whether the magic fires*. It is a gamble on *whether you can stay conscious long enough to control the aftermath*. That is a different, more interesting tension — and it puts the decision entirely in the caster's hands.

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**CASTING A PRINCIPLE**

1. **Declare** the Principle (School) and the Magnitude (1, 2, or 3).
2. **Pay the Agony Cost** — roll **1d4 Stress per Magnitude** and add it to your Stress Track immediately.
   - Mag 1: roll 1d4 Stress &nbsp;|&nbsp; Mag 2: roll 2d4 Stress &nbsp;|&nbsp; Mag 3: roll 3d4 Stress
3. **Make a Mind Save** against your new Stress total.
   - **Success:** You channel the Agony safely. The spell executes cleanly.
   - **Failure:** Take a **Rank of Suffering (Mental)**. Reset Stress to 0. The spell **still executes** — you're bleeding from the nose or screaming in silence, but the formula does not care about your condition.

</div>

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

*Vera needs to stop the guard captain from reaching the door. She declares **Principle of Kinetics, Magnitude 2** — Crush. She rolls 2d4 Stress: a 3 and a 1, total 4. Her Stress Track was already at 7. It's now at 11.*

*She makes a Mind Save, rolling 3 Mind Dice (she has d8). She rolls 4, 6, 2 — total 12. Her current Stress is 11. Success: she channels the Agony and the captain's knee buckles inward like wet paper.*

*She is rattled but standing. Next round, she needs to decide: keep the Stress building, or spend an Action to Vent.*

</div>

---

## VENTING AGONY

Accumulated Agony does not disappear on its own. An Esoterist who casts repeatedly without managing their Stress Track is walking toward a breakdown — not a dramatic one, but a sudden, absolute one.

**Vent** is the release valve. Spend an Action to purge the accumulated energy outward. Remove **1d6 Stress** from your track.

The cost: anyone within Near Range takes **2 flat damage** — ally or enemy. The psychic shockwave does not discriminate.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

*Venting looks different for every Esoterist. Some scream. Some go silent and the air pressure drops. Some Vent in complete calm — a breath out, a tremor in the hands, and then three people nearby are stumbling backward from a concussive wave they can't explain. The polite ones warn their allies first.*

</div>

# CHAPTER 2: THE MAGNITUDE SCALE

All Esoteria is measured on a three-point scale of intensity. Magnitude is not just a damage tier — it is a description of *how much of reality you are rewriting*. A Magnitude 1 effect nudges the physical world. A Magnitude 3 effect tears it.

| MAGNITUDE | SCALE | DAMAGE | DESCRIPTION |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1 (Tactical)** | Single Target / Small Object | 2d6 | One thing. One moment. Manageable. |
| **2 (Lethal)** | Small Group / Environment | 3d6 (Ignores Light Armor) | Several things at once, or one thing completely. |
| **3 (Catastrophic)** | Entire Zone | 4d6 (Ignores All Armor) | You are not casting a spell. You are rewriting a room. |

**Magnitude 1** is the daily language of the working Esoterist. Push a man back, flash a guard's eyes, age a lock to dust. The universe barely notices. The Agony is manageable. Field researchers cast Mag 1 a dozen times a day.

**Magnitude 2** is where the work gets serious. You are no longer nudging the physical world — you are breaking it. A Mag 2 cast withers a limb, crushes structural supports, fires a laser that punches through plate. The Agony bill is real. The look in your allies' eyes when you start bleeding from your ears is real.

**Magnitude 3** is the option you are not supposed to use. Gravity Wells. Decay. Arc Lightning chaining across three targets simultaneously. A Mag 3 cast in an enclosed space can end a mission — or start a different one. Esoterists who cast Mag 3 often find themselves in the Director's spotlight for reasons that have nothing to do with the original plan. *The spell always works.* What it does to the room is less predictable.

## RESISTING MAGIC

Magic hits automatically — but targets can attempt to survive the impact. When a Principle strikes, the target makes a **Save** against the Magnitude's Difficulty Rating.

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**Difficulty Rating (DR) = 10 + (2 × Magnitude)**

| Magnitude | DR | Feeling |
|:--|:--|:--|
| **Mag 1** | 12 | A shove from someone much stronger than you. |
| **Mag 2** | 14 | A car crash in slow motion. |
| **Mag 3** | 16 | Everything at once, all wrong. |

- **Target Success:** Half Damage, ignore secondary effects (Blind, Pinned, Weakened).
- **Target Failure:** Full Damage, full secondary effects.

The **type of Save** depends on the Principle: Kinetics hits Body, Entropy targets either Body or Spirit (Director's call by context), Luminescence typically hits Mind (the flash is as much psychic as physical).

</div>

# CHAPTER 3: THE PRINCIPLES

The Esoterist begins play knowing three **Principles** — the schools of Vardan geometric formula that form the backbone of practical Esoteria. Each Principle covers a domain of physics. Each has three Magnitude tiers with distinct effects.

Full spell lists for each Principle are detailed in the [Esoterist Archetype](../02_Character_Creation/01_Archetypes/ESOTERIST/) file.

### PRINCIPLE OF KINETICS (Force & Weight)
*The manipulation of momentum, mass, and gravity.*
The most intuitive Principle and the most commonly used in combat. Everything from a tactical push to a Zone-wide gravity inversion.

### PRINCIPLE OF ENTROPY (Decay & Time)
*The acceleration of rot, age, and dissolution.*
The most feared Principle by enemies and Directors alike. Entropy does not just damage — it *removes*. A target killed by Decay does not leave a body. An object struck by Rust does not leave wreckage. It was, and now it is not.

### PRINCIPLE OF LUMINESCENCE (Light & Energy)
*The focus and release of electromagnetic force.*
The Principle of pure energy. Flash blinds. Laser cuts through armor like it isn't there. Arc Lightning chains — one cast, three targets, all at full Magnitude.

# CHAPTER 4: RITUALS (CASTING OUTSIDE OF COMBAT)

Not all Esoteria is cast under fire. **Ritual Casting** is the slow, methodical application of Esoteric formula in a controlled environment — tracing geometries on a surface, speaking calibration phrases, measuring angles. It is the way Esoteria was originally practiced, before the Great War forced it into the trenches.

**Ritual Casting Rules:**
- Requires **1 full minute** (6 Rounds) of uninterrupted work.
- The caster chooses **Magnitude as normal** and pays the Agony cost.
- **No Mind Save is required** — the controlled environment allows the caster to manage the feedback safely.
- The effect is more precise: the caster may target specific features (a specific lock, a specific structural weak point, a specific person in a crowd).

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**If interrupted during a Ritual Cast**, the Esoterist has two choices:
1. **Abort** — lose the Agony cost already paid (Stress remains), no effect.
2. **Rush it** — complete the cast instantly as a combat action. Make a Mind Save at **+2 Difficulty** (the formula is imprecise). The spell goes off, but secondary effects are at Director's discretion.

</div>

**The Ritualist Talent** (Tier 1) allows an Esoterist to cast utility formulae — Silence, Message, Cleanse, Mend — for **0 Stress**, provided they take the full minute. This is the foundation of the Esoterist's non-combat toolkit.

# CHAPTER 5: THE SEVEN SEALS (MAGISTER RANKS)

To practice Esoteria is to exist within a hierarchy whether you want to or not. The **Schola Magisterium** is the governing body of licensed magical practitioners on the Shelf. They do not enforce compliance with kindness.

A licensed Esoterist carries a **Seal** — a numbered badge of rank, etched with their binding formula. An Esoterist without a Seal is, legally, a criminal. In practice, enforcement depends entirely on jurisdiction. In Novemgrod, unlicensed casting earns a fine and a warning. In parts of Ruthenia, it earns an execution.

**Advancement:** Rank advancement is a narrative milestone, not an automatic XP unlock. A Director awards a new Seal when the character has done something that the Schola would recognize — a significant field contribution, surviving a Backlash, publishing research. The mechanical benefits follow the rank.

| Rank | Title | Status | Mechanical Benefit |
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
| **1** | Neophyte | Newly initiated. Dangerous to yourself. | — (Starting Rank) |
| **2** | Apprentice | Survived first Backlash. Training under guidance. | **Hardened Mind:** +1 Die on Mind Checks to resist Agony. |
| **3** | Acolyte | Entrusted with dangerous texts and field work. | **Overcharge:** Once per Scene, increase a spell's Magnitude by +1 without paying extra Stress. |
| **4** | Journeyman | Independent contractor. Research allowed. | **The Binding:** Gain the Rite of Summoning. Can conjure Daemons. |
| **5** | Magus | True mastery. Schola contributor. | **Precision Casting:** Esoteria Skill Checks succeed on a roll of 4+ (instead of 5+). |
| **6** | Archmagus | Leads a Chapterhouse. Shapes the field. | **Cascading Power:** Once per Scene, a successful Esoteria Check causes the die to **Explode** (roll an additional die, add it to the total). |
| **7** | High Magister | One of Seven. Shapes the direction of magic itself. | **Perfect Mind:** Esoteria Skill Checks succeed on a roll of 3+. |

### ROGUE PRACTITIONERS

Esoterists who practice without a Seal — or who have had their Seal revoked — are called **Rogues**. The Schola uses a different word: *wraiths*. The implication is not accidental.

A Rogue is not automatically a villain. Plenty of unlicensed Esoterists are field researchers who couldn't afford the Schola's fees, or survivors of Chapterhouse collapses who never re-registered, or people practicing the old Vardan formulae that the Schola has formally classified as Forbidden.

What a Rogue is, mechanically, is **unprotected**. The Schola's training includes mental conditioning against the worst effects of Agony buildup. Rogues lack this. At the Director's discretion, a Rogue Esoterist who accumulates extreme Stress may face additional consequences on a failed Mind Save — hallucinations, identity fragmentation, or brief psychic broadcasts detectable by other practitioners.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

*In the old quarter of Novemgrod, unlicensed casting lights up like a flare to anyone carrying a Relic Compass. The Schola's retrieval teams call it "fishing." You walk the fog-line with a compass in one hand and a writ of arrest in the other, and you wait for someone to get desperate.*

*Most Rogues don't last long. Not because the Schola catches them. Because the Agony does.*

</div>

# CHAPTER 6: RELICS (MAGIC FOR EVERYONE ELSE)

You do not need to be an Esoterist to use magic, provided you find the right object.

A **Relic** is an item with a Principle formula permanently bound into its physical structure — inscribed into the metal, woven into the glass, burned into the wood. The Esoterist who made it paid the Agony cost at creation. The user who activates it pays a fraction.

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**USING A RELIC**

1. **Spend an Action** to activate the item.
2. The item casts its fixed spell at its fixed Magnitude. The effect is predetermined — you cannot adjust it.
3. **Take Flat Stress** equal to **2 × Magnitude** (no roll, just pain).
   - Mag 1 Relic: 2 Stress.
   - Mag 2 Relic: 4 Stress.
4. On a **Botch** (natural 1 on any die in the same round), the formula is **exhausted** — the Relic's binding collapses and it becomes inert.

</div>

**What Relics Look Like:** Relics are rarely labeled. A Mag 2 Kinetics Relic might be a perfectly ordinary-looking iron rod with geometric scratches on one end. A Mag 1 Luminescence Relic might be a glass eye that heats up when squeezed. Identifying a Relic's function without activating it requires an **Arcana or Lore check (DR 12)**. Activating an unidentified Relic is how people lose fingers.

**Who Makes Them:** The Artificer talent (Tier 1) allows an Esoterist to permanently bind a spell into an object at a cost of **3 Potential**. The binding process takes one Scene of uninterrupted ritual work. The resulting Relic can be given to anyone — the user takes the Agony, not the creator.

This is, economically, one of the most valuable skills a traveling Esoterist can have. A set of Kinetic Bullets or an Entropic Grenade is worth considerably more on the black market than the Esoterist's daily rate.

# CHAPTER 7: THE RITE OF VOLITION (DAEMON SUMMONING)

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

*"It is not a spirit. It has no soul. It is my will, given teeth."*

</div>

The word *Daemon* is a Schola term — clinical, intentionally stripped of the religious horror the common folk attach to it. What the Esoterist summons is not a demon in any theological sense. It is a **construct**: a semi-solid projection of focused Esoteric energy given a primitive instinct and a handful of directives.

Think of it as a very expensive, very temporary, very dangerous tool.

The Esoterist does not open a portal. They do not make a pact. They *build* the Daemon out of ambient Esoteric energy and a fragment of their own cognition — which is why Daemons have a faint reflection of their summoner's personality, and why the more powerful ones resent their existence. They are not borrowing someone else's will. They are meeting a compressed, aggressive fragment of their own.

### THE SUMMONING RITUAL

You cannot summon a Daemon in combat. The process requires **10 minutes**, a ritual circle (chalk will do, blood is better), and a drop of the caster's blood to anchor the construct's identity.

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**SUMMONING PROCEDURE**

1. **Choose the Tier:** Minor, Major, or Prime.
2. **Pay the Agony Cost:** Take the Flat Stress immediately (see table below).
3. **The Binding Roll:** Roll **ARCANA** vs. the Daemon's **Severity (DR)**.
   - **Success:** The Daemon is bound. It starts with **3 Favors**.
   - **Failure:** The Binding collapses. The energy dissipates. You take **1d6 Psychic Damage**. Try again (with more Stress already on the track).
   - **Critical Failure (natural 1):** The Binding **snaps**. The Daemon fully forms as a **ROGUE** — free, hostile, and permanent. You made a monster.

</div>

---

### THE DAEMON HIERARCHY

#### MINOR DAEMON — *The Fetch*
*What it is:* The simplest construct. Fist-sized or smaller. Most Fetches take ambiguous forms — a shape like a bird with too many joints, a shadow that moves when yours doesn't, a mass of pale filaments that slip under doors. They are not threatening. They are *wrong*.

* **Agony Cost:** 2 Stress
* **Severity (DR):** 12
* **Stats:** HP 6 | Damage d4
* **Favors:** 3
* **Capabilities:** Pass through gaps, carry messages, steal small objects, observe and report, unlock mechanisms from the inside.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

*You feel it leave. Not physically — you feel it leave the way you feel a thought leave when you're distracted. One moment you're holding its shape in your mind; the next, it's out there somewhere, doing what you told it. The sensory link is faint: impressions, not images. The smell of old paper. Cold stone. Movement in the dark.*

</div>

---

#### MAJOR DAEMON — *The Myrmidon*
*What it is:* A full-sized combat construct. Myrmidons are humanoid more often than not — the mind defaults to human scale when building something that needs to fight. They tend toward the featureless: a knight without heraldry, a soldier with no face, a shape that's almost a person from a distance and obviously isn't up close. The eyes, if they have any, are wrong. Too still. Too focused.

* **Agony Cost:** 4 Stress
* **Severity (DR):** 14
* **Stats:** HP 12 | Armor 1 | Damage d8
* **Favors:** 3
* **Capabilities:** Combat, heavy lifting, physical intimidation, guarding a position.

<div class="flavor-box" markdown="1">

*It doesn't breathe. That's the first thing people notice. You get used to the sound of breathing in a room — you stop hearing it until it stops. The Myrmidon stands at the door and the room gets very quiet. People tend to make eye contact with it by accident, and then spend the rest of the night not thinking about the way it looked back.*

</div>

---

#### PRIME DAEMON — *The Eidolon*
*What it is:* The limit of what a single mind can hold together without breaking. An Eidolon does not look human. It rarely looks like anything with a precedent. The shape shifts — not constantly, but enough that you can never fully describe it after. A report written by a Novemgrod Schola investigator on a rogue Eidolon incident described it as "approximately the size of a market stall, with a posture that suggested it was used to much smaller spaces." They never found the investigator's field notes. They found the investigator.

* **Agony Cost:** 6 Stress
* **Severity (DR):** 16
* **Stats:** HP 20 | Armor 2 | Damage 2d6 (Cleave — hits all adjacent targets)
* **Favors:** 3
* **Capabilities:** Siege-level combat, structural demolition, complex problem solving, holding an entire Zone.

---

### COMMANDING THE DAEMON (THE FAVOR SYSTEM)

A bound Daemon begins with **3 Favors**. Each command costs **1 Favor**.

Simple commands: *"Kill him." "Guard this door." "Unlock that vault." "Follow her and report back."*

**Remote Commands:** You can dispatch the Daemon to act autonomously at a distant location. This costs **2 Favors** — one for the travel, one for the task. While the Daemon is remote, you maintain a vague sensory link: impressions of its surroundings, not clear vision.

**Dissipation:** When a Daemon reaches 0 Favors, it dissolves. The steam smells faintly of copper and hot metal.

**The Test of Wills:** If you order a Daemon to do something that violates its basic programming (suicidal charge, action against its own form's instincts), the Director calls for a **Contested Willpower Check** (Mind vs. Severity DR). Failure means the Daemon refuses — and burns a Favor doing it. Repeated Tests of Wills on a powerful Daemon are how Rogue incidents start.

<div class="rule-box" markdown="1">

**GOING ROGUE**

A Rogue Daemon is not dormant. It is loose, hostile, and permanent — a construct with no master, no Favors remaining as a leash, and the full survival instinct of its tier. A Rogue Minor Daemon is an annoyance and a hazard. A Rogue Eidolon is a disaster.

The Daemonologist Talent (Tier 3) grants +1 Favor on all summonings (total 4) and **+1d6 on checks to Banish or Bind Rogue Daemons**. Directors are encouraged to make Rogue incidents feel consequential.

</div>

# CHAPTER 8: FORBIDDEN FORMULAE

The Schola Magisterium has classified certain Esoteric applications as **Forbidden** — not because they don't work, but because they work too well.

Forbidden Formulae are not included in Tier progression. They are found in the field: in sealed vaults, in the notebooks of dead Magisters, in the ruins of Vardan-era research stations. Using one draws Schola attention immediately. Being caught with the notation for one is a criminal offense.

The Director determines what Forbidden Formulae exist in their campaign. Examples:

* **SYMPATHETIC CASTING:** Casting a Principle through an effigy linked to a biological sample. Range becomes unlimited; Agony cost doubles. (Available as the Sympathetic Magic Talent, Tier 3 — even the Schola classifies this as forbidden-but-permitted-for-field-operatives-with-clearance.)
* **MEMORY EXTRACTION:** Entropy applied to neural tissue. Destroys the target's recall of a specific period. Leaves no physical evidence. The Schola calls it "irreversible cognitive salvage."
* **THE CONSUMPTION FORMULA:** An obscure Kinetics application that transfers Agony from one person to another. Requires physical contact. No one is sure who discovered it. Several people have tried to undiscover it.
